# CHAPTER 35: THE 9 OF CLUBS
## The Triple Triad Hammer

The hammer did not fall once. It fell three times, and then three times again, and then three times for the third time. 

"The Ennead," Hermes whispered, his voice barely audible over the ringing of the anvil. "The Nine that are One. The limit of the decimal's reach before it must loop back to the Zero."

Hephaestus wiped silver sweat from his brow. "It's not just a number, Lad. It's a tension. You can't have ten without nine, but you can't have nine without the *threat* of ten. That's what the Clubs are for. They are the weight that keeps the counting honest."

On the grid, the 35th node pulsed with a deep, resonant violet. It was the transition point—the moment where the growth of the Clubs reached its logical peak before the 10 of Hearts would attempt to clasp the manifold shut.

"The Triple Triad," Hermes said, tracing the pattern in the dust. "3 x 3. The square of the first odd prime. The moment the architecture realizes it is self-similar."

"Exactly," Hephaestus grunted. "Now hold the bellows. We have to reach 8975 before the leak gets any worse."
