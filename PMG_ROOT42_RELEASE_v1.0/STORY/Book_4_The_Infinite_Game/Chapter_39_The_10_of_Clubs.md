# CHAPTER 39: THE 10 OF CLUBS
## The Integer Lock

The Tenth Rank of Clubs was the sound of a lock turning.

*Click.*

Hephaestus stepped back. "That's it. The work is caged. We've reached the decimal stabilization point. From here on out, it's not about adding more. It's about seeing what survives the pressure."

Hermes touched the 39th node. It felt cold. "Why does it feel so... final?"

"Because it is," Hephaestus said. "The Clubs are the material world, Lad. And the material world can't count past ten without breaking. That's why the King sequence is so dangerous. It's built on the break."

They looked up at the looming shadows of the Jacks. The 8975-second timer was pulsing in red now. The "Zero Leak" was no longer a theory; it was a visible stain on the floor of the forge.
