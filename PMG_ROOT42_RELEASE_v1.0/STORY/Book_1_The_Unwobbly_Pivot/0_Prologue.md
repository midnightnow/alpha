# PROLOGUE: THE DREAM OF SISYPHUS

## The Dead Point
Every cyclist knows the dead point—the moment in the stroke where no force can be applied, where the leg must hang suspended. In the geometry of the soul, this is the **Pre-Session**. Sisyphus wakes in the dark, weightless in the infinite pour of choices. He has escaped his hill, but he finds he misses the density. 

## The Shop and the Stone
He finds himself in Kuta, at a shop called **Deus ex Machina**. Out of the corner of his eye—at the specific **42° refraction point**—he spies a small stone: the **Umber**. It is gritty, heavy, and real. He puts it in his pocket, plucking the real out of the noise of the **Ember**. 

## The Altar
In the corner of the bar, a man place a coin on a small altar. This is the **Sovereign Coordinate**. He is paying the **Accountants of the Tiny Unseen Angles** to keep the day from wobbling. Sisyphus realizes that if he leaves his hill, the world of meaning loses its anchor. He chooses to return. He chooses to be the **13th Post**.

## The Binding
He wakes on the hill. The dawn is grey. But his pocket is heavy with the Umber. He places his hands on the rock—the **5** meeting the **12**—and begins the climb. The coming equals the going. The pivot is unwobbly.
