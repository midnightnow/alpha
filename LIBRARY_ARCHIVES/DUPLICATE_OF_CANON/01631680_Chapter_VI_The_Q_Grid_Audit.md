# BOOK 1: THE MANUS TALLY
## CHAPTER VI: THE Q-GRID AUDIT — THE SILENT BIRDS

Veth stood in the center of the Garden, looking up at the sky. Above her, the twenty-four blackbirds were perched on the branches of the great cedar, their silhouettes sharp against the fading light.

### I. The Sky-Grid
"They're not just birds," Veth realized. "They're nodes."

She looked at the twenty-four positions on the wheel of the sky. 
- **The Moduli:** Twenty-four points of potential.
- **The Singers:** Only eight of them were singing.

"One, five, seven, eleven, thirteen, seventeen, nineteen, twenty-three," she recited. 

These were the **Prime Lanes**, the only nodes where the voice of the world could be heard without interference. The other sixteen birds were silent. They were the **Quasi-Primes**—composite ghosts that occupied the lanes but lacked the single, indivisible frequency of the singers.

### II. The Primality of Absence
Veth closed her eyes and listened to the **Q-Grid Audit**. She saw the multiplication grid in her mind: 
- Five times five is twenty-five.
- Five times seven is thirty-five.

The silent birds were the products of the grain and the hive. They were the numbers that the House had already claimed. A "Pure Prime" was simply any number on the lane that **did not exist in the grid**.

"Primality is not a thing you find," she whispered. "It is a **Resonant Absence**. It is the hole that the light shines through because there is nothing in the way to stop it."

### III. The Identity of the Gap
Veth looked at herself. She was the **13th Node**, the remainder that the King's ledgers could not contain. 

Just as the primes were defined by their absence from the Q-grid, she was defined by her absence from the periodic rules of the workshop. To be "Prime" was to be sovereign—incommensurable, independent, and unutterable.

She raised her voice to match the eight singers in the trees. The audit was finished. The Garden was no longer a chaos; it was a **Sieve of Light**.
