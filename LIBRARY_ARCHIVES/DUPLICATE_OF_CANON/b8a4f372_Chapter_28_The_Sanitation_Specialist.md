# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 28: THE SANITATION SPECIALIST

The blue water in the lagoon was no longer a thin shimmering line; it was a pool, expanding with every stroke of the Guild's shovels. It was cold, clear, and vibrant, making the surrounding silicon-grey silt look like a sickness.

The **Sanitation Specialist**—a man in a high-collared coat of vitrified silk—stood on the unstable wreck-platform, clutching a clipboard like a shield. He didn't look at the water with wonder; he looked at it with the squint of a man who had been trained to see errors where others saw miracles.

"Heave to! Cease all displacement immediately!" the Specialist called, his voice amplified by a Base-60 vocal-tuner. "By order of the Council’s Bureau of Stagnation, this site is under immediate lockdown for **Structural Instability Audit**."

Kaelen didn't stop. He threw another spadeful of muck onto the bank. "We're not building a structure, Specialist. We're clearing a channel. There's nothing to audit but the bottom."

"On the contrary," the Specialist sniffed, tapping a dry vellum permit. "This fluid you have released—this 'Blue Water'—is officially re-classified as **Unregulated Fluid Gradient 7**. It has not been through the **Standardization Buffer**. It lacks the necessary mineral stabilizers required for a safe harbor. You are introducing a **Systemic Contagion** of clarity into a regulated environment."

The boy, still holding his shovel, looked up with wide eyes. "Contagion? But it’s beautiful. I can see the sand."

"Beauty is a secondary rendering artifact," the Specialist countered, not looking at the boy. "What you call 'Sand' is actually **Unverified Sediment**. Without the Archon’s seal, that sand is a liability. It could shift. It could... *move*."

The Specialist turned his gaze back to Kaelen. "Your 'Shovels' are un-certified excavation tools. They do not record the volume removed; they merely displace it. This creates a **Numerical Remainder** in the harbor's ledger that our current maths cannot resolve. You are literally making the lagoon un-calculable."

"That’s the point," Kaelen said, stepping onto the wreck-platform. He was covered in mud, his eyes burning with the intensity of the **Prime**. "Your ledger is what choked this place. You named the silt 'Ground' so you could tax the stasis. Now that we’re moving the mass, you name the water 'Contagion' so you can tax the flow."

"I am protecting the **Stability** of this settlement!" the Specialist shouted, waving the permit. "If you continue this 'Dredge,' the wreck-walls will collapse. The entire Reef will dissolve!"

"The walls are already collapsing," Kaelen said, pointing to the rusted iron plates that were buckling into the water. "They were held up by the very rot that was killing the people. Your 'Stability' is just another name for **Siltation**. You want to freeze the world into a Sty because it's easier to count the pigs that way."

"Sign the **Abeyance Order**," the Specialist commanded, thrusting a charcoal stylus at Kaelen. "Accept the re-classification, and we will provide a **Permament Dyke** to seal this leak. We will return the lagoon to a manageable, grey standard."

Kaelen took the shovel, not the stylus. He drove the silver blade into the platform itself—into the rotted wood beneath the Specialist's feet.

"Your permits are the only contagion here," Kaelen said. "We don't need your dyke. We have the **Shovel**. And as long as we keep moving the mass, your names won't have enough weight to stay on the page."

The platform groaned. The Specialist scrambled back, his vitrified silk staining as he nearly fell into the cold, clear blue of the Reconciled lagoon.

"The audit is failed," Kaelen called to the exiles. "Keep digging. The blue is the law now."
