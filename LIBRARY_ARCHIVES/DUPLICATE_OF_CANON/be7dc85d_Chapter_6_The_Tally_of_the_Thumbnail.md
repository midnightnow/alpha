# CHAPTER SIX: THE TALLY OF THE THUMBNAIL

*The High Street / The Battlements / The 13th Node*

"The King thinks he has me trapped in a row of thirteen posts," I said, placing my elbow on the stone of the northern battlement. "He counts the intervals and sees a straight line that never ends."

I didn't push the wall; I swung against it. I used my forearm—the **Historical Cubit**—as the radius of a new world. As my hand traced the arc, the 13th Node didn't disappear; it became the **Pivot**. 

The linear "Grave" of the city wall collapsed into the circular "Garden" of my reach. The Hired Man, standing beside me, held his fifty-one-centimetre rule like a priest holding a censer. 

"The wall doesn't fall because of the height," he said, his voice a low vibration that matched the stones. "It falls when we forget the gap. The gap is where the centre carries the weight. The gap is All Ways Here Now."

I placed my hand on the thirteenth stone—the corner merlon, the one the mason always left slightly out of true. My thumb-stop was the final boundary. My fingers were the twenty-six letters of a language the guards couldn't read.

"I am the End Effect of the city," I whispered. "The overshoot. The 13th harmonic. Not a prisoner of the King's twelve-foot law—the part of the frequency that the law had been generating all along without knowing it."

I turned my hand over. Palm down—the **Chalice**, receiving the cold frequency of the wall. Palm up—the **Crown**, radiating. This was the **Executive Flip**. The wrist, the Hidden Cross, performed the 90° transition between receiving and radiating.

Beneath us, the High Street ran its four-interval length between five lines of posts. From up here, it looked exactly like the fence it was. From down there, it had looked like a row of graves. 

The weight of the city was held by the gaps. The life of the city was held by the silence between the King's lines.
