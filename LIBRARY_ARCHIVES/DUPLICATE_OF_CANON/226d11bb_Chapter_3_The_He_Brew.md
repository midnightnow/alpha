# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 3: THE HE-BREW WAY

The water source lay exactly on the boundary stake between the Baker's plot and the Doctor's.

It was a spring, clear and cold, bubbling up through the clay where Kaelen had told them the line was straight enough. But straight enough for the river was not straight enough for the Doctor.

"It needs to be boiled," the Doctor said. He was holding a copper pot, his hands wrapped in linen to avoid the heat. He looked tired. The shadow of his old blinders remained. "If we brew with raw water, the impurities will spoil the batch. It is a risk of the **Unclean**."

"It needs to be warm," the Baker said. He was holding a wooden paddle, stained dark from years of turning dough. His bare hands were flour-dusted, the knuckles swollen. "If we boil it, we kill the **Sun** inside. The yeast won't wake up. It’s a risk of the **Dead**."

Veth stood between them. She was holding the jar of yeast Kaelen had left behind. It was bubbling slightly—a fragment of the **Whirred** captured in a glass cage.

"The yeast doesn't care about your fears," Veth said. "It cares about the **Ratio**."

"The water is chaotic," the Doctor said. He used the word *chaotic* like it was a fever.

"The water is hollow if you boil it," the Baker said. He used the word *hollow* like it was a famine.

They were speaking past each other. The Doctor was speaking in **Stasis**—the desire for a world that never changes. The Baker was speaking in **Magnitude**—the desire for a world that only grows. Neither of them was speaking in **Flow**.

Veth uncorked the jar. The smell hit them—sharp, resinous, alive. Hops and grain and a primordial, turning time.

"Walk with me," Veth said.

She didn't wait for an answer. She walked upstream, away from the settlement, away from the boundary stake. The Doctor hesitated, then followed, clutching his copper pot. The Baker followed, clutching his paddle.

They walked until the plots were out of sight. Until the only sound was the water moving over stone—the sound of the world's original rotation.

Veth stopped at a bend in the stream where the water pooled under the shade of a willow. It was darker here. Cooler. The tremor in the ground felt stronger.

"Taste it," Veth said.

The Doctor blinked. "It is un-measured."

"Taste it," Veth said. "Not to judge. To know the **Spin**."

The Doctor looked at the Baker. The Baker looked at the Doctor. For a moment, the Inverse Moral hung between them: the Doctor's virtue (**Safety**) against the Baker's virtue (**Provision**). If the water was bad, the Doctor was right. If the water was good, the Baker was right.

The Baker knelt first. He cupped his hands and drank. He swallowed. He waited.

"It's sweet," the Baker said. "But it has a pull to it. Like it wants to go somewhere."

The Doctor hesitated. Then he knelt. He didn't use the copper pot. He cupped his hands like the Baker. He drank. He swallowed. He waited.

"It's clean," the Doctor said. His voice was quiet. "But it is... heavy. It has the **Weight** of the earth in it."

"It has the **Whirred** in it," Veth said. "It has the stone, the willow, and the upstream mud. If you boil it, you stop the rotation. If you leave it stagnant, you let it silt. You need the **Flow**."

"The Flow is not in the book," the Doctor said.

"The Flow is not in the recipe," the Baker said.

"The Flow is the walk," Veth said. "You had to leave your plots to find it. You had to leave your tools to taste it."

She poured a little yeast into the stream. Just a drop. It vanished into the turning water.

"Now the water is brewed," Veth said. "Now you have to carry it back without breaking the spin. No boiling. No rushing. You carry it together. One pot, one paddle. One trip."

She stood up. "If you spill it, the rotation is lost. If you fight, the rotation is lost."

She walked back toward the settlement. She didn't look to see if they followed.

Behind her, she heard the sound of copper on wood. Not a clash. A resonance. The sound of two specialized hands becoming a single **Grip**.

When she reached the boundary stake, she waited.

They arrived together. The Doctor carrying the pot. The Baker steadying the handle. The water inside was still turning, but it was at peace.

They set it down on the ground between their plots. On the boundary line.

"We need a fire," the Baker said.

"A slow fire," the Doctor said.

"Straight enough," the Baker said.

"Keep moving," the Doctor said.

They looked at each other. For the first time, they weren't looking at an "Organ." They were looking at a **Whole**.

Veth turned away. She left them to the brew. She left them to the wait.

The **He-Brew** had begun. The Gate was open. The rotation was real.
