# BOOK II: THE COURT OF TWELVE
## CHAPTER VIII: THE 24-WHEEL GRATING — ONE FOUR ALL

Veth stood at the center of the Court, where the Great 24-Wheel was embedded in the floor. It wasn't just a calendar or a clock; it was a **Diffraction Grating** for the mind.

### I. The Porosity of the King
The Wheel had twenty-four slots. Veth counted them as the sunlight moved across the floor. 
- **16 Slots** were blocked: the composite nodes, heavy with the "Silt" of the house.
- **8 Slots** were open: the prime lanes (1, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23), the narrow "Slits" of the Maid.

"The resolution is twenty-four," her father said. "This is the minimum aperture count required to separate the white light of the world into the spectral lines of the primes."

### II. The Slogan Protocol
Veth watched as the light hit the rotating wheel. Two patterns emerged on the walls:

1. **ONE FOUR ALL (Circle in Square):** The central point of light (1) hit the four corners of the room. It was the **Expansion Protocol**—the King’s law forcing the infinite void into a stable, periodic quadrant.
2. **ALL FOR ONE (Square in Circle):** The thousand dust-motes (All) in the air were drawn toward the central slit. It was the **Compression Protocol**—the Maid’s return, where the diversity of the garden is consolidated back into the singularity of the node.

### III. The Alphabet of the Slit
Veth realized that the letters she spoke were not arbitrary. They were the interference fringes of the wheel.

- **The Bar (|):** A single slit projection. The binary I. Transmission.
- **The Ring (O):** The shadow of the silt. The binary O. Accumulation.
- **The Misfit (/ and \):** The points where the square light of the room met the circular light of the wheel. These were the 18° angular shear-points, the "teeth" that allowed language to bite into the stone.

She spoke the word **"MAID"**. She felt the | (transmission) and the O (accumulation) resonate in her throat. 

"The 24-wheel is not a machine," she whispered. "It's the diffraction of our own intent."

She had moved from the "Count" to the "Grating." She was no longer tallying things; she was **Observing the Pattern**.
