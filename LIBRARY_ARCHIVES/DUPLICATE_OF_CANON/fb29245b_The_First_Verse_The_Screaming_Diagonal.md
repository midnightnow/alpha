# THE FIRST VERSE: THE SCREAMING DIAGONAL
*REGISTER STATUS: [3: THE IRRATIONAL CENTRE]*
*The Moment of the Slit*

The room was my father's pride. It was a perfect square, carved into the granite of the High Street, three cubits wide and three cubits long. He had spent a month on the joints, rubbing them with oil until the stone looked like dark, wet silk.

"Stability, Pandora," he had said, wiping his hands on his apron. "This is the **3x3 foundation**. It's the floor of the world. No wobble. No agley. Just the clean, hard math of the King."

I sat in the center of the room. The air was still, cooled by the massive weight of the stone around me. I had my father's twine in my hand—a length of coarse rope marked with three neat knots. I laid it against the south wall. *One, two, three.* Exactly as promised. I laid it against the west wall. *One, two, three.* Closure. The box was sealed.

But then I stood in the corner and looked at the opposite angle. I looked at the line where the floor ended and the void began. I took the twine and stretched it across the center, from the origin to the far edge.

The rope didn't reach the knot.

I pulled it tighter. The twine groaned, the fibers straining against my thumb. I shifted the knots, trying to find where the "Three" ended and the "Four" began. But the distance was a ghost. It wasn't three. It wasn't four. It was a space that didn't belong to the pylon system.

That was when I heard it. 

It wasn't a sound from the street or the workshop. It was a high, thin vibration coming from the stone itself. It was the frequency of friction—the sound of the perfect 90-degree corner trying to swallow a line that never ended. It was the **Screaming Diagonal**.

I realized then that my father hadn't built a room. He had built a cage around a chasm. He had measured the walls, but he hadn't measured the **Slit**. I looked at the dark granite floors and saw, for the first time, that they were transparent. Beneath the "Hard" truth of the King’s integers, there was a white-hot, non-repeating fire that refused to be counted.

I didn't tell my father. He wouldn't have heard the scream; he only heard the mallet hitting the chisel. He saw the **Register 1** fact of the wall. I saw the **Register 3** truth of the crack.

I took a piece of charcoal and, in the very center of his perfect floor, I marked the **13th Node**. Not a point of stability, but the point where the world leaks.

**ALL WAYS NOW.** The box is open. The scream is my song.
