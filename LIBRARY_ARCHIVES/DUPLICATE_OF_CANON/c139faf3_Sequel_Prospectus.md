# Sequel Prospectus: SEXAGESIMAL

**Subtitle:** *Terrible to Behold: The Secret History of Biblically Accurate Angles*

## The Premise
While the Olympians were busy obsessing over the Grid and the Perfect Square, they forgot about **Gaia**. She has been ignored for eons, treated as mere "flooring" for their architecture. Bored and melancholic ("The Malaise of Gaia"), she decides to make things interesting: she bends the perfect circular orbits into **Ellipses**.

## The Conflict

*   **The Math Breaks:** There is no simple Euclidean formula for the perimeter of an ellipse. The Grid cannot calculate it. The Olympians panic.
*   **The Season Patch:** To manage the fluctuating distance from the sun (perihelion/aphelion), the gods are forced to invent **"Spring"**—a coiled dimension of time that allows the earth to spiral rather than cycle.
*   **The Villain/Hero:** To compress this new, complex "Spiraling Time" into human minds, they introduce **Base-60 (Sexagesimal)** math.
*   **The Horror:** When humans look at Base-60 geometry, they don't see shapes; they see **Ophanim**—fractal wheels of eyes spinning through the sky.

## The Tagline
*"They never draw me from my good side. I just wish, for once, people would remember my better angles."* — Lucifer (Light-Bearer / Geometry Teacher)

## Book 3 Setup
The sequel ends with the stabilization of the Spiral, but leaves a box in the corner labeled "Pandora / Hope / Entropy."
