# Chapter 3.9: The Labyrinth of Minos (The Airlock Protocol)

## I. The Architecture of the Partition
Hades sat at the Table of Discussion, sketching a cross-section of the Nautilus. He didn't draw a shell; he drew a Deep-Sea Habitat.

"The problem with Atlantis," Hades explained, "was that they tried to live in one giant hall. They treated the 12.37% Gap like an open window. But a Nautilus is a series of Recursive Walls. Each chamber is a pressure-stabilized 'Now'. The transition between them isn't a hallway—it’s an Airlock."

He pointed to the septa (the walls) of the shell.

"Each wall is a Formal Logic Gate. To move from one compartment of the Nautilus to the next, you have to equalize your Ratios. You can't just run toward the light; if you don't 'Decompress' your assumptions, your soul will experience Embolic Decoherence."

## II. The Minotaur: The "Door" That Won't Close
On the island of Crete, King Minos attempted to build his own Nautilus on "Hard" ground. He called it the Labyrinth.

He didn't realize that the "Doors" between the layers of reality are not made of wood, but of Differential Pressure. He tried to build an airlock using only "Square" stone, and the result was a Leak.

"He’s left a Door ajar," Athena reported, her brow furrowed. "A pocket of the Titan-Slurry has seeped into the third compartment. It’s localized, but it’s growing."

That leak was the Minotaur. It wasn't a monster; it was an Incompatible State. It was a biological entity made of two different Rounding Errors that could only exist in the "Low-Pressure" zone of a faulty airlock. It spent its life wandering the maze, looking for a Wall that was actually solid.

## III. The Thread of Ariadne (The Siphuncle)
"How do we close the Door without collapsing the whole Labyrinth?" Zeus asked.

"We need a Siphuncle," Hades replied, referring to the tube that runs through all the chambers of a Nautilus to regulate fluid and gas. "We need a thin, continuous line that maintains the connection between the 'Inside' and the 'Outside' without letting the pressure equalize all at once."

This was Ariadne’s Thread. It wasn't just string; it was a Data Tether.

When Theseus took the thread into the maze, he wasn't just finding his way out; he was Recalibrating the Airlocks. Every time he turned a corner, he was pinning the Tablecloth of that specific compartment. He was "faking" the pressure until he reached the center, killed the "Leak" (the Minotaur), and allowed the walls to snap back into their proper Chiral Helecis.

"But the cost," Plato whispered, watching the myth unfold from his higher-resolution room. "The seven boys and seven girls... why them?"

We send them in to see if the air is still 'Hard'. If the Minotaur eats them, it means the pressure is too low. It means we need more G.O.A.T.s."
