# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 14: THE GUILD'S GAMBIT

The Guild of Brewers (Tal's former masters) approached Kaelen. They offered to "standardize" the Garden's brewing process. In exchange, they want exclusive rights to the yeast strains.

"We can scale this," the Guildmaster said, unrolling a parchment covered in Base-12 calculations. "Standardized vats. Regulated fermentation. Quality control. We'll ensure every drop of Bir meets the spec."

Kaelen looked at the parchment. He saw the tollgates. The valves. The specified channels of communication.

"And if the Guild falls?" Kaelen asked.

The Guildmaster blinked. "The Guild doesn't fall. The Guild is the memory of the craft."

"The Guild is a single point of failure," Kaelen said. "You're asking us to trade resilience for efficiency. You're asking us to become an organ in your body."

Tal stepped forward, holding a jar of wild yeast. "This yeast doesn't care about your spec. It cares about the air. The air is free. The air is everywhere. If you gate the air, the yeast dies."

"We're offering order," the Guildmaster snapped.

"We're offering life," Tal said. "Life is messy. Life is distributed. Life doesn't have a single point of failure."

Kaelen rolled up the parchment. "The Garden doesn't need a Guild. It needs a network. Teach the craft. Then move on. If you stay, you become the Archon."

The Guildmaster's eyes twitched. He saw the Chit-Cascade collapsing. He saw the Ghost Assets evaporating. He saw the Hired Man refusing to be kept.

"You're building a system that can't be controlled," the Guildmaster said.

"We're building a system that doesn't need to be controlled," Kaelen said. "The Wolf will audit it. The River will refresh it. The People will maintain it. We don't need a Guild. We need each other."
