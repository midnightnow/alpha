# Diagonal Rupture: The $\sqrt{2}$ Displacement

In standard 3D space, we perceive the "short sides" (the $x$ and $y$ vectors of a floor). Gravitational force acts perpendicular to these. However, the **hypotenuse** ($\sqrt{2}$) represents a direction that is neither purely horizontal nor purely vertical—it is the vector of **displacement**.

* **The Theory:** Gravity binds the "sides" (the mass). Antigravity must inhabit the "diagonal" (the field).
* **The Contemplation:** By vibrating a craft at a frequency relative to $\sqrt{2}$, we are attempting to move "between" the grid lines of the $1 \times 1$ physical reality.
