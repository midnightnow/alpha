# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 19: THE SPRING AT THE PEAK

The climb was a physical manifestation of the **Inverse Moral**. 

The Baker, optimized for the heat and stationary weight of the oven, found the thin, mountain air a violation of his "Mass." His heart hammered against his ribs like a trapped bird. 

The Doctor, optimized for the sterile precision of the clinic, found the irregular terrain of the ridge a "Diagnostic Failure" of the earth. His knees, locked by years of sitting at the examination desk, shrieked with **Precipitated Torque**.

They were carrying the heavy oaken bucket between them, a communal anchor.

"Slow... down," the Baker wheezed, his face the color of a raw beet. "The gravity... it’s out of spec."

"It’s not the gravity," the Doctor rasped, clutching the other side of the handle with white-knuckled intensity. "It’s your... respiratory efficiency. You’re over-ventilating. It’s... inefficient."

"Shut up," the Baker said, but they didn't let go of the bucket. If one dropped it, the other would fall.

They reached the crest of the ridge where the spring bubbled out from beneath a slab of black basalt. The water didn't look like "Liquid Assets" or "Sterile Medium." It looked like **Cold-Sharp**.

"Touch it," Kaelen had said.

The Baker knelt, his flour-dusted knees sinking into the wet moss. He plunged his hands into the pool. "Cold," he whispered. "Bitter-cold."

The Doctor knelt beside him. He adjusted his slipping spectacles and touched the surface. "Wet," he said. "And... clear. I can see the stones at the bottom. They look like... **Sweet-Stone**."

The "Specialist spectalcles" fell from the Doctor's face and splashed into the water. He didn't reach for them. He looked at the Baker, his eyes wide and unfiltered.

"Do you feel that?" the Doctor asked.

The Baker looked at his own hands, the "Sealer's Claw" starting to twitch in the icy current. "The Hiss... it’s quiet. Not gone, but... quiet."

"It’s the **Primitive Flow**," Kaelen said, appearing at the edge of the rocks. "You’re not 'Baking' or 'Healing' right now. You’re just **Carrying**. Your virtues have found a bottom because the water doesn't care about your labels."

They filled the bucket together, a single **Pair of Hands (10)**. As they turned to begin the descent, the Baker slipped on a wet stone. The Doctor reached out and caught his arm—not as a medical intervention, but as a reflex of the whole.

"Got you," the Doctor said.

"Thanks," the Baker replied. "Neighbor."

The descent was still an agony, but the **Warp** in their bodies started to find a new alignment. They weren't fighting the terrain; they were using it. The **Vine** was growing.
