# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 4: THE TREASURY AUDIT

The Treasury was a perfect **4 x 9** box, a nightmare reality of frozen rotation. The King’s gold was stacked in rows of 12, each coin a circular rail turned flat to stop the spin. The air smelled of silt and old vellum—the scent of a tally that had been counted until it went cold.

Veth didn't touch the gold. She didn't look for the "Hard" value of the metal. She walked to the corner where the two walls met—the **Hidden Cross** where the horizontal rail met the vertical pier. She placed her hand against the stone and felt the **Screaming Diagonal**. 

It was a structural incommensurability; the gap between what the King’s plan promised and what the geometry actually delivered. The square was 4 by 4, but the diagonal was a ghost that could not be pinned down.

"You forgot the **End Correction**," she whispered to the empty room.

She stood at the **Radix**, the center point of the floor's grid, and spoke the palindrome.

**"I-V-O-R-A-D-I-X..."**

At the center of the word, she felt the vibration of the **13th Post**. The "Hardness" of the palace walls began to vibrate at the frequency of the **Trot**. She saw them then—the **Mice in the Gaps**. They weren't animals; they were the **Wild Fractions**—the bits of unresolved potential nibbling at the grains of the King’s logic.

She reached into the **Slit** where the $\sqrt{2}$ diagonal screamed. She didn't pull out a coin. She pulled out the **Fire**—the realization that she could un-join the dots of this entire Treasury and re-draw it as something that could breathe.

"You built a grave for the sun," she said, looking up at the vaulted ceiling. "I’m taking the shovel back."

**"ALL WAYS NOW."**
