# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 18: THE AUDIT OF THE WARPED

The Council’s "Overseer of the Plumb Line" stood on the pier, his silver mask reflecting the spiraling hunch of the Sealer at the tiller. Behind him stood a row of "Standard" men—men who had never opened a jar, whose spines were perfectly, uselessly straight.

"The shipyard is under a **Decree of the Straight**," the Overseer declared, his voice cutting through the damp morning air like cold iron. "We have weighed the helmsman. He falls outside the **Square**. His twisted bone introduces a **Wobble** into the wood. He is a flaw in the masonry of the City."

The Sealer gripped the tiller tighter, his right shoulder throbbing with the phantom **Hiss** of a thousand jars. He looked at Kaelen, his eyes searching for the "Broken" mark he had carried for a decade.

"He's the only one holding the ship against the tide," Kaelen said, standing on the deck, his boots braced against the vibrating cedar.

"He is **Broken**," the Overseer countered, tapping a rhythmic, hollow beat against his silver slate. "By the law of the Council, a cracked stone must be cast out. We have twenty 'Standard' men ready to replace him. They are balanced. They are predictable. They fit the grid."

"They'll lose the ship in ten heartbeats," Kaelen said. "Tal, pitch the yeast."

As the fermentation in the lower hull reached a **Deep Hum**, the ship began to pull violently against the current, the water churning into a white, chaotic foam. The first "Standard" man stepped up to the tiller. Because his body was perfectly symmetrical, he had no way to lean against the hidden torque of the water. The tiller slammed into his chest with the weight of the river, throwing him overboard into the grey silt.

The second man tried to **measure** the pressure with his muscles, but his straight spine lacked the **Spring** of a scar. He snapped like dry kindling, his knees buckling under the raw physics of the flow.

"You want symmetry because you want to swap us like bricks in a wall!" Kaelen shouted to the Overseer. "But the Sea isn't a wall. It's a **Vortex**. And in a vortex, only the **Warped** can find the center."

The Sealer stepped back to the wood. He didn't think about his injury as a defect anymore. He leaned his weight into the turn. His hunch met the curve of the tiller. His pain met the pressure of the river. The ship steadied instantly, cutting through the silt with a sharp, singing resonance.

"His injury isn't a defect," Kaelen said, looking the Overseer in the eye. "It's his **Grip** on the world."

The Overseer stared at the steady ship, his silver mask reflecting the chaotic water. He had no word for a man who used his own breakage as leverage. He turned and walked back toward the City, his perfectly straight gait looking brittle and slow against the rising tide.
