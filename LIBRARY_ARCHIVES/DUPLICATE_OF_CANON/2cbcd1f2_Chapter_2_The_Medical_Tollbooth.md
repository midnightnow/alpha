# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 2: THE GATE OF THE BLIND

The Gate was a crude frame of saplings, but it was draped in the heavy, light-blocking tapestries of the Old City. It smelled of ozone and stale air—a pocket of the static dark held together by sheer denial.

"Stop," a man said. He wore a heavy leather apron and held a pair of smoked-glass lenses to his eyes. He was the Overseer of the Plumb Line. "You’re squinting. Your eyes are unbalanced, girl. The Whirred is spinning your mind too fast. You have **Too Much Light**."

"I’m not squinting," Veth said, clutching her stick. "I’m seeing."

"It’s the same thing in this light," the Overseer replied, his voice clipped and hollow. "The rotation of the world is grinding your spirit to dust. You are becoming **Hollow**. You need to be weighted down. You need to return to the **Rocky Stance**."

He held up a pair of iron blinders. They were heavy, cold, and dark. They were designed to press the wearer’s gaze into the dirt, to match the **External Pressure** of the Council's will.

"Put these on," the Overseer urged. "The world will stop screaming. You’ll be able to sleep again. You’ll feel heavy, solid, predictable. You won't feel the **Tremor** anymore. You'll be a stone in the wall instead of a leaf in the wind."

Kaelen stepped forward. "And the price?"

"The price is your **Spin**," the Overseer said, looking at Kaelen through his dark lenses. "You admit you cannot stand the Whirred. You admit that the Council’s **Measure** is the only safe ground. You stop pushing out, and you let us push in. We do the watching; you do the walking."

"You're selling lead as a cure for the sun," Kaelen said. "You want to keep her small and rocky so your gravity can still hold her down."

"I'm selling **Stasis**," the Overseer corrected. "The Whirred is a furnace, Kaelen. It will ignite you. It will turn you into a Star, and you will burn out alone in the void. Put on the iron. Be a subject again. Be safe."

Kaelen looked at Veth. He saw the tremor in her hands—the same tremor that was in the ground beneath them. 

"Don't take the iron, Veth," Kaelen whispered. "The pressure is only heavy because you haven't started spinning fast enough to push back. Ignite. Become the Sun."
