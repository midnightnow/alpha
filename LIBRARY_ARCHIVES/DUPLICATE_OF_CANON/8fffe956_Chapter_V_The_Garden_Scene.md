# BOOK 1: THE MANUS TALLY
## CHAPTER V: THE GARDEN SCENE — THE BINARY JUMP

The workshop door creaked open, and for the first time, Veth stepped out of the periodic safety of the house.

Behind her, the floor was a predictable tiling of squares and hexagons—the **Bread and Honey** of her father's craft. Four sides for the home, six sides for the hive. They locked together without a single gap. But the path before her, leading into the Wild Garden, was different.

### I. The Step into the Five
The stepping stones were cut into pentagons. Veth looked at them and felt a sudden, sharp vertigo. 

"They don't fit," she whispered.

"No," her father said, standing at the threshold. "Pentagons cannot tile the plane. To walk this path, you must accept the **Shear**. You must accept the gaps."

Veth took the first step. Her foot landed on the five-fold stone, and the ground seemed to shift. It wasn't a solid lock; it was a **Joined Choice**. This was the **Agley Path**—the movement that occurs when the count of the fingers (5) meets the wildness of the world.

### II. The Binary Jump ($2^n$)
As she walked deeper into the garden, she realized that the distance wasn't stretching out in a straight line. It was scaling.

"Step one," she counted. One flower.
"Step two." The patch.
"Step four." The layout.
"Step eight." The entire estate.

Each step was a doubling, a **Binary Jump** that transformed her perspective. She wasn't just walking; she was **Scaling the String**. By jumping in powers of two, she could see the structure of the landscape without needing to count every blade of grass. The garden was no longer a collection of things; it was a **Reciprocal Map**.

### III. The Scent (0.2696...)
She paused and inhaled. The air was thick with a scent that had no name in her father's ledger. It wasn't the sweet, heavy smell of the Parlor's honey. It was something sharper, more volatile.

"The remainder," she murmured.

It was the **0.2696 Scent**—the alternating sum of prime reciprocals, the "wildness" that the King's pavement could never cover. It was the evidence that the Garden was infinite, aperiodic, and alive.

Veth looked back at the house. The periodic order was a small square in her vision now. Before her lay the infinite complexity of the five-fold path, vibrating with the scale of the jump.

**REMAINDER: SCENTED.**
**JUMP: BINARY.**
