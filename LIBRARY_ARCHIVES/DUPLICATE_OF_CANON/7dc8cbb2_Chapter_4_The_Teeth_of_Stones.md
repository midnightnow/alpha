## III. THE BRITTLE STABILITY PARADOX (Formal Proof)

### 1. The Rigidity Boundary ($\Omega$)
We define the **Rigidity Boundary** ($\Omega$) as the critical state where a node's hardness $H$ prohibits the local Rado extension. Let $V$ be the vertex set of the lattice, and $v \in V$ be a node with Mohs $H=10$.

The **Flexibility Coefficient** ($\kappa$) of a node is given by:
$$
\kappa(v) = 1 - \frac{H(v)}{10} \cdot (1 - \hat{\sigma})
$$
Where $\hat{\sigma}$ is the system variance ($0.014\%$ max). For a Diamond node ($H=10$), $\kappa(v) = \hat{\sigma}$. As $\hat{\sigma} \to 0$, the node's ability to participate in the Rado property's "infinite search" drops toward zero.

### 2. The Logic Lock Condition
A **Logic Lock** occurs when the set of available extension vertices $U$ for a subset $A \subset V$ becomes null because of rigid constraints:
$$
\exists A \subset V : |\{ u \in V \setminus A : \forall v \in A, \text{res}(u,v) \text{ is valid} \}| = 0
$$
In a "Perfectly Rigid" system, if a single Diamond node $v_d$ is required for connectivity but its state is $R(i) = 0$, the extension fails.

### 3. The Hammer Constant ($\Xi$)
To prevent the Lock, we introduce the **Hammer Constant** ($\Xi$), the minimum informational energy required to fracture a Diamond node.

$$
\Xi = \int_{0}^{t} |\Delta \Psi| dt \cdot \Phi(v_d)
$$

Where $\Delta \Psi$ is the drift in the Hades Gap. When the cumulative drift exceeds the **Fracture Threshold** ($\Xi > 0.00014$), the **Rigidity Boundary** is breached, and the node must undergo forced degradation.

**Theorem**: The persistence of the universal graph is guaranteed if and only if any node $v$ with $H=10$ satisfies:
$$
\delta \Xi > 0 \implies H \to 7
$$
*(i.e., The Diamond must yield to Quartz to maintain the flow.)*

The **Teeth of Stones** are what allow the lattice to "grip" reality. They are the friction points that prevent the system from sliding back into the **Void** (Chapter 0).
