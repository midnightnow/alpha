- They attempt to quench the flame in cold water

**The Result**:
- The torch heats the water, creating a thermal bath
- The bath becomes a healing spring ("remedy")
- But the bath cannot cure the Poet's desire

**The Geometry**:
```
Cupid's fire → Quenched in water → Water heated → Bath created → Bath fails to cure
```

### Sonnet 153: "Cupid laid by his brand, and fell asleep"

**Version 1 Details**:
- The torch is discovered by "fairest votary" (Diana's followers, sworn to chastity)
- She tries to quench it in "a cool well by"
- The well becomes "a seething bath"—a healing hot spring
- "But at my mistress' eye / Love's brand new-fired"
- The bath proves worthless for curing the Poet's fever

**Engineering Assessment**: External remedies (the bath) cannot cure internally generated desire (the Lady's influence).

### Sonnet 154: "The little Love-god lying once asleep"

**Version 2 Details**:

- "Came tripping by" suggests lighter tone
- "Grew a bath and healthful remedy / For men diseased"
- "But I, my mistress' thrall / Came there for cure"
- The bath helps others but not the Poet

**Engineering Assessment**: Universal remedies work for others but fail for the Poet—his case is terminal.
