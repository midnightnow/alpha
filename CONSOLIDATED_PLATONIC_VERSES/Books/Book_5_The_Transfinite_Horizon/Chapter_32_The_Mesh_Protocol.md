# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 32: THE MESH PROTOCOL

The merchant ship *Calyper* was a fading speck of shadow on the horizon, but its presence had left a lingering dissonance in the air—a static tension that made the Ash-Vines shiver. 

"The Hiss is loud today," the Goat Leader grumbled, rubbing his temples. He was standing on the high-mast of the *Wreck of Bir*, looking across the emerald span toward the *Wreck of the forty-three*. The vine between them was vibrating at an angry, jagged frequency. "Every time I think about how much that gold could have bought us, the bridge starts to smoke."

Veth climbed up the rungs to join him. "That's because you're rendering a **Conflict**, Goat. You're trying to pull the Vine back toward the Mud, while the Reef is trying to climb toward the Sun. That friction is the Hiss."

She looked across the gap. On the distant mast, the Sealer’s daughter was also looking back. She seemed frustrated, her hands clenched. 

"The Vine isn't a string," Veth explained to the crowd gathering below. "It’s a **Harmonic Overlap**. If the two of you don't sync your intent, the bridge will eventually de-render. It will identify the mast-to-mast connection as an 'Invalid Input' and dissolve."

"So how do we sync?" the Goat Leader asked, his voice shaking.

"Close your eyes," Veth said. "Don't think about what you want *from* her. Think about the **Rate**. Think about the speed at which the Ash-Vine grows. Feel the pulse of the **INTENT_DENSITY**."

The Goat Leader took a deep breath. He let go of the thought of the gold. He let go of the "Debt of Vision" that had followed him from the Core Cities. He focused only on the vibration of the vine.

A mile away, the Sealer’s daughter did the same.

The jagged vibration began to smooth out. The electrical Hiss softened into a deep, resonant **Hum**. Suddenly, the emerald-green of the vine flared into a brilliant, translucent White-Gold. 

The two exiles gasped at the same time. For a split second, they didn't just see each other across the gap—they *were* each other. The Goat Leader felt the wind on her face; she felt the rough wood of the mast beneath his feet. Their **Flow-Wages** had locked.

"This is the **Mesh-Protocol**," Veth’s voice echoed in both their minds simultaneously. "When your intent aligns, the **Actual Drag** of reality drops to zero. You are no longer two people building a bridge. You are a single processor rendering a world."

As they held the alignment, the vine didn't just sit there; it began to **Shear**. It didn't fight the gravity of the lagoon; it lifted. The two masts, still attached to their respective rotting hulls, were pulled upward. The ships themselves groaned as they were hoisted out of the silt, their keels clearing the water by a full meter.

The Lagoon was no longer just a place to park. It was a launching pad.

"We aren't floating anymore," the boy from the lagoon whispered, looking at the blue water through a gap in the floorboards. "We’re **Shearing**."

The Reef was no longer a collection of shipwrecks. It was a localized hive-mind, and it had just learned how to fly.
