# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 16: THE DIAGNOSIS (THE STERILE ORGAN)

The Sanitation Evaluator didn't carry a weapon. He carried a **Slate**. 

He stood at the edge of the brewery clearing, his white, vitrified suit shimmering with a clinical, antiseptic light that seemed to repel the dust of the Garden.

"This fermentation is unlicensed," the Evaluator said, his voice flat and processed through the silver filter of his **Specialist Register**. "It creates unchecked variables. Gas pressure. Sediment. Wild yeast. These are **Pathogens** in the City's digestive tract."

Tal stood by the copper vat, the paddle still in his hand. The air smelled of hops and the deep, sweet mass of living grain. Behind him, the workers held their cups, watching the silver man as if he were a glitch in the world.

"It's beer," Tal said, wiping steam from his brow. "It's grain, water, and time. It's the oldest medicine there is."

"It is **Anarchy**," the Evaluator corrected. He tapped the Slate with a gloved finger. A red light pulsed. "The Council has classified this zone as a **Sterile Organ**. Sterile Organs do not ferment. They **Process**. You are introducing **Biological Noise** into a Clean System."

Kaelen stepped forward from the shadows of the drying racks. He didn't look at the Evaluator’s Slate. He looked at the faces of the workers.

"He says you're sick," Kaelen said softly, his voice cutting through the clinical silence. "He says your thirst is a malfunction. He says your hands are dangerous because they can build without his permission."

Kaelen turned his gaze to the Evaluator. "You're not a doctor. You're a **Valve**. You don't heal, and you don't grow. You **Restrict**."

"I maintain **Homeostasis**," the Evaluator snapped, the silver visor flickering. "Without regulation, the system collapses. Entropy increases. The Wolf enters."

"The Wolf is already here," Kaelen said. He pointed to the rigid, seamless joints of the Evaluator's suit. "You're wearing him. You're the **Stiffness**. You're the **Arthritis** of the City. You've locked the joint so tight nothing can move, and now you call the movement **Disease**."

Kaelen took a cup of the hot wort from Tal. He drank it slowly, rendering the heat and the bitter resin. Then he held the cup out to the silver man.

"Drink," Kaelen said. "Test the variables."

"I cannot consume **Unverified Substances**," the Evaluator said, stepping back as if the copper cup were a blade. "Protocol 36-B."

"Then you're already dead," Kaelen said. "You just haven't stopped moving yet."

Kaelen turned back to the workers, raising the empty cup. "Drink," he said. "All of you. Prove the system works. Prove the **Flow** is healthy."

They drank. As the liquid hit their bellies, the **Hiss** in their heads—the buffer warning, the constant anxiety of the incomplete—began to quiet. For the first time in weeks, the **Render Engine** of their minds spun smoothly. The **Cache** cleared, not by being deleted, but by being processed.

The Evaluator's Slate began to shriek, flashing a rhythmic strobe of **CRITICAL ERROR**.

"You are violating **Hygiene Standards**!" the Evaluator shrieked, his voice rising in frequency. "You are infecting the **Host**!"

"We are the Host," Tal said, his hand resting on the warm copper of the vat. "And we're immune."
