# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 2: THE GATE OF THE BLIND

Gate was a crude frame of saplings, draped in heavy, light-blocking tapestries of Old City. It smelled of ozone and damp loam—a pocket of heavy dark held by a grim denial.

"Halt," a man barked. He wore a heavy leather apron, pressing smoked-glass lenses on a brow. He was Warden of a Plumb Line. "You're peering. A look is unbalanced, girl. Whirred is whipping a mind too fast. You have Too Much Light."

"I'm not peering," Veth said, gripping a staff. "I'm plumbing."

"Same thing in this glare," Warden replied, a voice blunt and hollow. "A grind of a world is milling a spirit on loam. You become Hollow. You need weight. You need Marrow-Weight."

He held up a pair of iron blinders. Heavy, cold, and blunt. Forged to press a look in mud, matching External Force of Council grip.

"Don these," Warden urged. "A world will stop throbbing. You'll be able to rest. You'll feel heavy, firm, bound. No feeling a Quiver. You'll be a block in a berm rather than a leaf in a gale."

Kaelen stepped forward. "And a burden?"

"Burden is a Turn," Warden said, peering through dark glass. "You admit you cannot bear Whirred. You admit Council Law is a only base ground. You stop driving out; you let us drive in. We do a keeping; you do a treading."

"You're offering lead as a balm on a sun," Kaelen said. "You want her dim and hard so your weight can bind her."

"I'm offering Plumb-Weight," Warden corrected. "Whirred is a furnace, Kaelen. It will ignite you. It will turn you on a Star, and you will burn out alone in a void. Put on iron. Be bound. Be bound."

Kaelen looked on Veth. He saw a quiver in a palm—same quiver in mud beneath.

"Don't take iron, Veth," Kaelen whispered. "A force is only heavy because you haven't begun beating deep enough to drive back. Ignite. Become a Sun."
