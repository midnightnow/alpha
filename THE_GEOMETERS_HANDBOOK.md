**Tools:** Straightedge, Compass, Marking Tool.

### **Sequence 2: The 3-4-5 Minimal Closure**
**Goal:** To move from 1D counting to 2D structure by finding the smallest integer "Right-Angle" closure. This demonstrates the **Prime Post Rule** ($a^2 = 2b + 1$) in action.

#### **Construction Steps**
1.  **The Vertical Post (a=3):** Draw a vertical line. Mark 3 equal intervals. 
    *   *Count:* 3 intervals, 4 boundary points.
2.  **The Orthogonal Turn (90°):** At the base, strike a perpendicular line (the "Road"). 
    *   *Utility:* This is a choice to break 60° symmetry for linear construction.
3.  **The Horizontal Road (b=4):** On the road, mark 4 equal intervals.
    *   *Count:* 4 intervals, 5 boundary points.
4.  **The Hypotenuse Closure (c=5):** Connect the top of the Post (3) to the end of the Road (4). 
    *   *Verification:* This length will precisely equal 5 units. $3^2 + 4^2 = 5^2$.
