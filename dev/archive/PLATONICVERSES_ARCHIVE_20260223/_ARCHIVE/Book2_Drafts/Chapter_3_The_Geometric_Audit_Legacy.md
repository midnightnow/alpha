# Chapter 3: The Geometric Audit

## I. The Standard Model

Minos didn't just look at the wave; he checked the **Phase Alignment**.

"You think you are being judged on your deeds," Minos said, adjusting the focus on his oscilloscope. "You are not. You are being judged on your **geometry**."

He pulled up a holographic wireframe. It wasn't human. It was a template. A figure standing in a robe, one hand raised in benediction, the other holding a crystal orb.

"This," Minos said, "Is the **Salvator Mundi**. The Savior of the World. Or, as we call it in the server room, the **Checksum**."

He tapped the chest of the hologram. Two crossed straps of embroidery formed a distinct 'X'.

"Do you see that angle?" Minos asked.

Agathon squinted. "It looks like an X."

"It is **138.157 degrees**," Minos recited. "It is the geometric complement of the Great Pyramid. It is the angle of the **Rhombic Triacontahedron**. It is the angle at which protein folds without breaking. It is the angle of Mercy."

## II. The Square Man

Minos turned the knob, overlaying Agathon's soul-data onto the template.

It didn't fit.

Where the template flowed at a graceful 138.157°, Agathon's data jagged sharply at **90 degrees**.

"You are orthogonal," Minos observed with distaste. "You spent your whole life trying to be upright. Rigid. Square."

"I was a man of principle!" Agathon argued. "I stood straight!"

"You stood at a Right Angle," Minos corrected. "In a spherical universe, a Right Angle is an abomination. It requires infinite energy to maintain. You were fighting the curvature of space itself."

Minos pointed to the red flashing digits on the screen: **THD: INFINITE**.

"Nature grows at **137.5 degrees**—the Golden Angle," Minos explained. "The Divine Template allows for **138.157 degrees**. But you... you insisted on **90 degrees**. You tried to build a grid on a ball. No wonder you're exhausted."

## III. The Tuning Fork

"And then there is the sound," Rhadamanthus added, covering his ears.

Minos nodded and tapped a tuning fork against the console. It hummed with a warm, round, golden tone.

"This is **432 Hertz**," Minos said. "The frequency of the Earth. The spin of the sphere. The hum of a happy cat."

He then played Agathon's soul-file. It shrieked. It was sharp, anxious, and tight.

"You are tuned to **440 Hertz**," Minos noted. "Standard Concert Pitch. The frequency of the Air Raid Siren. The frequency of military bands. It is 8 cycles faster than reality."

"I was motivated!" Agathon cried. "I was high-energy!"

"You were sharp," Minos said, stamping the paper. "You were vibrating 8 times per second faster than the universe intended. That isn't 'high energy,' Agathon. That is just **Anxiety** encoded as a waveform."

## IV. The Verdict

Minos handed the slip to the shade.

"Tartarus?" Agathon asked, his 90-degree shoulders slumping.

"No," Minos said. "The Re-Tuning Room."

"Is that... painful?"

"Only if you stay stiff," Minos said, reaching for the next file. "We're going to put you in a room playing a 432 Hz Sine Wave until your corners melt off. It usually takes about three aeons."

He looked at the long line of rigid, square, 440 Hz souls stretching back to the horizon.

"Next."
