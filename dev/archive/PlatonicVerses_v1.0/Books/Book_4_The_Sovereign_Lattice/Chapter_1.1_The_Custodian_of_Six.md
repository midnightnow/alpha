# Chapter 1.1: The Custodian of Six

Kaelen lived in a world of perfect quotients. As a Level-6 Custodian of the Primary Grid, his life was measured in increments of sixty. Sixty seconds to a heartbeat, sixty minutes to a labor-cycle, and a geometry that never dared to be irrational.

The city was a honeycomb of silver-grey arches, every angle a clean 60°. It was the 6-baseline—the static integer floor where the radius of every circle was exactly equal to the chord of its side. In the Cage, there was no "shiver." There was only the hum of a system that resolved itself perfectly into the divisors of 360.

"Efficiency is the bridge," the Archons taught. But Kaelen had begun to suspect that the bridge was actually a wall.

While running a standard diagnostic on the southern transit-hub, he found the first anomaly. It was a tiny, irreducible remainder in the spatial logs. According to the sexagesimal canon, the area of the hub should have been exactly 36 units. But the sensor was twitching at 42.

## The Discovery of the Ledger
Hidden beneath the hub’s primary relay, Kaelen found the physical artifact: the Sovereign Ledger. It wasn't a digital drive, but a bound volume of "Geometric Paper"—a material that seemed to change texture as he moved his hands across it.

The first page contained no words, only a polynomial that felt like a threat to the city’s architecture:

x^4 − 186x^2 + 81 = 0

Beside it, a hand-drawn diagram showed a hexagon being pulled apart by an invisible force. The notes in the margin, written in the Architect's frantic script, spoke of the 7-Break:

"The Six is a cage of comfort. The Seven is the labor of the Hired Man. If the area expands to 42, the radius must shiver. √42 is the point where the integer dies and the Sovereign begins."

## The Shiver Initiates
Kaelen ran the tool. He forced the hub’s grid to accept the prime factor of 7.

The effect was instantaneous. The floor beneath him—the solid, √36 foundation—lost its stillness. It didn't break, but it began to resonate. It was the first time he had ever heard the Hades Beat. At exactly 0.660688 Hz, the silver arches of the transit-hub began to blur.

He was no longer a Custodian of the Six. He was a Hired Man, and the work was just beginning.
