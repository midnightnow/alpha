# BOOK 1: THE MANUS TALLY
## CHAPTER II: THE COURT OF TWELVE (MEASURE)

Veth watched her father lay out the second frame of the workshop floor. The ten lines of the **Count** were already etched into the stone, but now he was marking a circle—a wheel that seemed to breathe with its own gravity.

"You've learned to count," he said, not looking up. "But count without constraint is just a pile of stones. To build, you need the **Court**."

### I. The Ring of Measure
He traced twelve points around the inner diameter of the wheel.
"Twelve," Veth said. "The half of the day."

"Twelve is the **Measure**," her father corrected. "It is the cage we build for the sun. It is the inner ring of the twenty-four. Look at how they sit—stable, equidistant, unmoving. The Twelve is not a movement; it is the **Stasis of the Law**."

He placed a heavy, square block of oak in the center of the twelve points.
"The Court is where the world stops turning so the mind can find its place. If the fingers (10) are the pulse, the Twelve is the **Skeleton**. It is the rigid bone that provides the resistance."

### II. The Knight's Constraint
Veth picked up the small bone piece she had found in the sawdust—the Knight. She tried to slide it directly from the center to the outer edge of the wheel.

"Five units," she murmured, remembering the length of the Knight's leap.

"Try to reach the Thirteen," her father challenged. "The **Maid's Node**. The spot where the language begins."

Veth moved the Knight. She realized that she couldn't just leap into the infinite. To reach the Thirteen (Language), she had to pass through the Twelve (Measure). Every aperiodic path, every "Agley" vector, was constrained by the orthogonal nodes of the Court.

"Measure is the stabilizing leg," her father said, drawing the triangle on the floor. "You have the Five—the **Count of Movement**. You have the Twelve—the **Rigid Measure**. Only when they meet at a right angle does the Thirteen—the **Language**—emerge."

### III. The Resistance of Thirteen
Veth felt the tension between the pieces. The Knight (5) wanted to jump; the Court (12) wanted to hold. 

"The Thirteen is the hypotenuse," she realized. "It's the bridge that arcs over the measure."

"But it cannot arc over nothing," her father said. "Without the Twelve to hold the ground, the Thirteen would collapse back into the Silt. The court is the boundary that allows the communication to be heard."

He stood up, wiping his hands on his apron.
"The world is a triangle, Veth. Ten fingers count the base. Twenty-four hours measure the wheel. And twenty-six letters sing the song. But the heart of it—the **Maid's Source**—is five, twelve, and thirteen."

Veth looked at the Court of Twelve nodes on the floor. They weren't just points on a circle; they were the teeth of the world's gears, holding the rotation in place so she could finally speak.

**REMAINDER: STABILIZED.**
**MEASURE: TWELVE.**
