# BOOK 1: THE MANUS TALLY
## CHAPTER I: THE COUNT (10)

The world began at the fingertips.

Veth sat on the dusty floor of the workshop, her legs crossed, watching the sun cut a path through the sawdust air. She held her hands out, palms up. Ten digits. Ten nodes of engagement.

"Count them," her father said, his voice as rough as the cedar he was planing.

"One, two, three..." Veth began.

"No," he interrupted, leaning over her. "Don't just name them. Feel the **Base**. Your hands are not just tools for holding; they are the **Ordinary World's Tally**. They are the first law of Ten that the mind uses to keep the Void at bay."

### I. The Ten and the Void
Veth looked at her fingers. Ten. The decimal origin. 
"Why ten?" she asked.

"Because the Zero is too hungry," her father replied. "The Zero is the mouth of the abyss. The Ten is the fence we build around it. With ten, you can count the coins, you can count the days, you can count the grains in a sack. You can pretend the world is an integer."

He picked up a piece of charcoal and drew a single line on the floor—a vertical post.
"A single post. One."

He drew another next to it.
"Two. A duality. The first strobe."

He continued until there were ten lines.
"The Tallyman's Limit. This is where most men stop. They think that if they can count to ten, they have measured the world. But the Ten is just the **Count**. It is the pulse, not the path."

### II. The Arising of the Angle
Veth touched the ten lines. They felt stable, rigid. But then she looked at her own hand. The thumb didn't align with the fingers. It sat at an angle—a vector of potential.

"The thumb is the five," she whispered.

"The thumb is the **Agley**," her father corrected. "The thumb is what turns the square hand into a tool of intent. It is the five that reaches for the diagonal."

He took his square and laid it across the ten lines. 
"Look at the corner, Veth. See the ninety-degree rightness? That is the **Joint of Civilisation**. But watch what happens when the Ten tries to turn that corner."

He drew a base of six units and a height of eight. 
"Six, eight... what is the diagonal?"

"Ten," Veth said instantly.

"Exactly," he said, his eyes gleaming. "The Ten is not a flat line. In the real world, the Ten is the **Hypotenuse of the doubled triad**. It is the bridge between the horizontal silt and the vertical vision. Your ten fingers are not just a count; they are the evidence of a hidden triangle."

### III. The First Handshake
Veth realized that her two hands, when clasped together, formed a single origin. $5 + 5 = 5$. The ten was a doubled five, a symmetry that broke the flat counting of the integer world.

"The Count is the beginning of the Language," she realized.

"And the Language is the final Measure," her father added. "But first, you must learn to trust your hands. They are the only rulers that don't lie. They contain the math of the Maid before you ever open a book."

Veth looked back at her ten fingers. They were no longer just digits. They were the first coordinates of a vast, emergent lattice. The world hadn't just begun; it had **Aligned**.
