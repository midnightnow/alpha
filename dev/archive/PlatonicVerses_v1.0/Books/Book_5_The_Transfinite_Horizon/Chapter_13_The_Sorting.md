# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 13: THE SORTING

The Priest stood at the edge of the harbor, his shadow stretching across the vitrified wharf like the finger of an auditor. He held a silver **Caliper** in his left hand and a wooden **Paddle** in his right—the two registers of the City's soul.

Below him, the **Ship of Bir** was a riot of activity. The crew was dredging the last of the silt from the rudder housing, their movements jerky and "Warped" but filled with a purpose that the Priest’s ledger couldn't quite contain.

"You are trying to sail on the **Irrational**, Kaelen," the Priest said, his voice a perfect **$\sqrt{36}$** resonance that seemed to flatten the ambient noise of the harbor. "You’ve filled your hold with a brew that refuses to be measured, and your crew is made of the **Warped** and the **Asymmetrical**."

Kaelen didn't stop the dredging. He signaled to the Sealer to tighten the main-sheet. "We’re just exploring the proportions, Priest. The 'Hard bit' is here, and your 'Hard Supports' are already silting up. We're launching because the stable is no longer safe."

"I am here to sort the **Pros** from the **Cons**," the Priest replied, stepping closer to the edge. He pointed his silver caliper at the Doctor, who was currently hauling a bucket of mud from the hold. "This man was an 'Organ' of the City. A specialist of high order. Now he is a 'Barbarian' with dirt under his nails. Is that a Win or a Theft?"

"It’s a **Refresh**," Kaelen said, looking up. "He’s no longer 'Normal' by your standards, Priest, but he finally has a **Grip**. He’s learned that the 'Good' of the Liver isn't found in a book or a register, but in the **Wait** of the ferment. He isn't a Con; he's becoming a **Professional** who can actually move the load."

The Priest looked at the bubbles rising from the fermentation vats in the hold—the living **Hiss** of the yeast reacting to the harbor air. He smiled—a look that carried both the weight of a blessing and the sharp edge of a warning.

"Then go," the Priest said, lowering his tools. "But remember: the Wolf is an honest auditor. He doesn't care about your register or your Latin labels. He only cares if your **Warp** is strong enough to hold the **Weft** when the vortex comes. If you fail, Kaelen, the remainder will be the end of you all."

"If we fail," Kaelen said, "the remainder will be the truth. And we're okay with that."

He signaled the launch. The Priest watched as the ship of contradictions wrenched itself from the docks, a fragment of the Garden breaking free from the Sty.
