# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 7: THE SIGNATURE OF THE MANUS

Veth stood atop the 12-foot outer wall of the City State. From here, the High Street looked like a sequence of rigid, gray beads on a string—a string that the King and his Priests were pulling taut until it hummed with the tension of debt.

The wind whistled through the gaps in the battlements—the **End Effect** notches that the Masons had been forced to leave. It was a high, singing note that the guards called a warning. Veth knew it for what it was: the **13th harmonic** trying to escape the 12-foot tallness of the masonry.

She looked at her hand, her **Manus**. Five fingers, one **Agley** thumb. She realized that her own arm was the pipe, and her hand was the radiation at the end of it.

"The King signs with a seal," a voice said behind her. It was the Hired Man. He stood with his feet planted wide, his own central spline aligned with the axis of the gatehouse. In his hand, he held a **51-centimeter rule**. "The King thinks his signet makes the law. But a Mason signs with the **End Effect**. We leave the 13th post slightly out of phase, so the wall can breathe."

"But the guards are still there," Veth said, looking down at the spears tracking her movement. "The 90-degree box is still closed."

"Only if you see it as a box," the Hired Man said. He placed his own palm over the stone notch. "Place your hand here. Use the **Cubit** of your intent."

Veth placed her palm beside his. She didn't seek to push the stone. She sought to **Fret** it. She felt the vibration of the city’s standing wave—the overlapping interference of the Market, the Church, and the Palace.

She spoke the operator, the 26-letter verse of the hand.

"*M-A-N-U-S-X-D-I-G-I-T-U-S-X-I-V-O-R-I-X...*"

As she spoke the reflection, the "Hard" granite beneath her palm changed its frequency. The Dirge of the King’s tax—that heavy, sinking 12-unit thrum—was suddenly "overwritten" by the song of the **13th Node**. 

The battlements didn't change their shape, but their **Executive Decision** flipped. The wall was no longer a cage; it was a **Vibrating String**, and she and the Hired Man were the ones holding the frets. She saw the **Hidden Cross** at the center of the city rotate, the orthogonal streets flickering as they aligned with the **Hex-Mountain**.

"The wall isn't high," Veth whispered, her hand radiating the white fire of the **End Correction**. "It’s just long. And we’ve just found the end of it."

The Hired Man nodded. "All ways now. The intent overshoots the bone."

Veth looked out over the city. She wasn't an inhabitant anymore. She was the **Transducer**. The High Street wasn't a road; it was a verse. And she was the one with the hand on the string.
