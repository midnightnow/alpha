## II. The Prime Number Invariants

### A. The 6k ± 1 Constraint
The project relies on the structural sparsity of prime numbers.
*   **Theorem:** For any prime $p > 3$, $p$ can be expressed in the form $6k + 1$ or $6k - 1$ for some integer $k$.
*   **Proof:** Any integer can be represented as $6k, 6k+1, 6k+2, 6k+3, 6k+4,$ or $6k+5$. 
    *   $6k, 6k+2, 6k+4$ are divisible by 2.
    *   $6k+3$ is divisible by 3.
    *   Therefore, all primes $> 3$ must reside at $6k+1$ or $6k+5$ (equivalent to $6k-1$).
*   **Application:** This is the bedrock of the "Wall." Primes are not random; they are confined to specific structural "lanes" relative to composite multiples.

### B. The Alternating Prime Reciprocal Series (The Leibniz Walk)
The core rhythm of the narrative is modeled on the alternating sum of prime reciprocals.
*   **Formula:** $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{p_n} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{11} - \dots$
*   **Convergence:** Unlike the divergent series of prime reciprocals ($\sum \frac{1}{p_n} = \infty$), this alternating series converges to the constant $\approx 0.269606...$ (the Flajolet–Vardi constant variant).
*   **Application:** This provides the "Breathing Mechanism" of the Ouroboros. It guarantees that despite infinite primes, the alternating tension between Expansion (Verb) and Contraction (Noun) remains strictly bounded and converges to a stable limit.

## III. Modular Arithmetic and the Geometric Grids

### A. The 24-Modulus Theorem (The Wheel)
The 24-Wheel is the primary container for the project's symmetry.
*   **Theorem:** For any prime $p > 3$, its square is congruent to 1 modulo 24. 
    *   $p^2 \equiv 1 \pmod{24}$
*   **Proof:** 
    1.  If $p > 3$ is prime, then $p$ is not divisible by 2 or 3.
    2.  Thus, $p = 6k \pm 1$.
    3.  $p^2 = (6k \pm 1)^2 = 36k^2 \pm 12k + 1 = 12k(3k \pm 1) + 1$.
    4.  Since either $k$ or $3k \pm 1$ must be even, $k(3k \pm 1)$ is a multiple of 2.
    5.  Therefore, $12k(3k \pm 1)$ is a multiple of 24.
    6.  Hence, $p^2 - 1$ is a multiple of 24.
*   **Application:** This proves that all primes $> 3$ "lock" into a 24-fold rotational symmetry. It validates the 24-Wheel not as an arbitrary choice of hours in a day, but as a fundamental topological feature of prime numbers.

### B. The 120-Modulus Extension (The Castle Floor)

*   **Theorem:** For any prime $p > 5$, $p^2 \equiv 1 \pmod{120}$.
*   **Proof:** Similar to the modulo 24 proof, factoring in the constraint that $p$ is not divisible by 5. 
*   **Application:** 120 is exactly $5 \times 24$. In the narrative geometry, multiplying the structural wheel (24) by the operational step of the Hero/Maid (5) scales the system from a flat circle into a fully stable 3D operational volume (The Castle/Icosahedron framework).
