## 1. THE RESONANCE DRIFT
**Critique:** "Superconductivity ($Z \to 0$) is tied to 'Perfect Resonance' with the Hades Gap. However, the existing Rado toolkit establishes that $\Psi$ is constantly drifting. This implies that the Superconductive State is inherently unstable and should be modeled as a **Transient Pulse** rather than a persistent state."
**Requirement:** Implement a "Coherence Window" for superconductivity that closes as $\Delta \Psi$ increases.

## 2. EROSION VS. PETRIFICATION
**Critique:** "You define Intent Decay as $I(t) = I_0 \cdot e^{-\lambda t}$. In Chapter 12 (Nativity), you imply that petrification stops this decay. What happens if a node is semi-manifested? Does the decay rate slow down, or is it binary?"
**Requirement:** Define the interaction between Intent Strength and the **Petrification Coefficient** from the Mineral Operator.

## 3. SIGNAL TELEPORTATION PARADOX
**Critique:** "You mention 'teleporting' intent through the H3 grid. In a physical GIS system, even instantaneous propagation must respect the **Topology of Neighbors**. A signal cannot jump to a distant H3 cell without passing through the intermediate ones, even at $Z=0$."
**Requirement:** Clarify that 'teleportation' is logical (zero latency) but still respects the adjacency of the Rado Graph extensions.

---

**Verdict**: The "Conduction of Intent" adds a necessary layer of agency, but the engineering needs to account for the instability of the superconductive mode.
