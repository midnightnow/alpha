📜 The 2017 Discovery
"Babylonian trigonometry is exact, whereas we are accustomed to approximate trigonometry."

— Dr. Daniel Mansfield, UNSW

In 2017, mathematicians Daniel Mansfield and Norman Wildberger demonstrated that Plimpton 322 represents the world's oldest trigonometric table—predating Greek trigonometry by over 1,500 years. The Babylonians used an "exact, angle-free" approach based on ratios of sides, avoiding the approximations inherent in angle-based systems.

The tablet originally contained 38 rows; this fragment preserves 15. Using base-60 (sexagesimal) arithmetic, every ratio terminates exactly—no irrational numbers, no rounding errors.
