- **Date**: c. 1800 BCE (Old Babylonian period)
- **Origin**: Likely Larsa, southern Mesopotamia (modern Iraq)
- **Current Location**: Rare Book and Manuscript Library, Columbia University, New York

## Key Scholarly Interpretations

### 1. Trigonometric Table Interpretation (Primary Source for This Visualization)

**Mansfield, D.F., & Wildberger, N.J.** (2017). "Plimpton 322 is Babylonian exact sexagesimal trigonometry." *Historia Mathematica*, 44(4), 395-419.

- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.hm.2017.08.001
- **Key Argument**: The tablet is an exact ratio-based trigonometric table, superior to modern tables because it uses exact ratios rather than irrational approximations.
- **Innovation**: Introduces "spread" as an alternative to angle measurement.

> *"Plimpton 322 is the world's oldest and most accurate trigonometric table."*

---

### 2. Scribal Exercise / Pedagogical Interpretation

**Robson, E.** (2001). "Neither Sherlock Holmes nor Babylon: A Reassessment of Plimpton 322." *Historia Mathematica*, 28(3), 167-206.

- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1006/hmath.2001.2317
- **Key Argument**: The tablet was a teacher's aid for generating mathematical problems involving reciprocal pairs in base-60.
- **Context**: Part of the OB (Old Babylonian) scribal curriculum for training accountants and surveyors.

> *"Plimpton 322 is a product of a very particular mathematical culture."*

---

### 3. Systematic Generation Method

**Britton, J.P., Proust, C., & Shnider, S.** (2011). "Plimpton 322: A Review and a Different Perspective." *Archive for History of Exact Sciences*, 65(5), 519-566.

- **DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1007/s00407-011-0083-4
- **Key Argument**: The triples were generated using a systematic algorithm, possibly involving reciprocal pairs and "regular" numbers.

---

### 4. Original Publication

**Neugebauer, O., & Sachs, A.** (1945). *Mathematical Cuneiform Texts*. American Oriental Society, New Haven.

- **Historical Note**: First scholarly publication and analysis of Plimpton 322.
- **Discovery**: The tablet was acquired by George Arthur Plimpton in 1922-23 from Edgar J. Banks.

## The Three Lenses

This visualization offers three interpretive frameworks:

| Lens | Interpretation | Primary Advocate |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| **Builder** | Practical catalog of construction slopes | Mansfield & Wildberger |
| **Trig** | Trigonometric table of ratios | Mansfield & Wildberger |
| **Number** | Scribal exercise in regular numbers | Robson |

All three perspectives have scholarly merit; the truth likely involves elements of each.
