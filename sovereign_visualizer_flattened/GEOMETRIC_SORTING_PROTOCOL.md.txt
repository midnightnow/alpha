**Phase: The Horizon Scan of the Paddock**
*“All the problems of math apply directly to file sorting, book writing, editing, and consolidation.”*

## I. AXIOMS OF THE FILE SYSTEM
Before we can sort the paddock, we must define the geometric nature of the digital environment. 

1. **The Queue (Files):** Files are literal queues—linear streams of 1s and 0s intersecting along a one-dimensional path.
2. **The Fold (Folders):** Directories function as folds in space, pleating the queues into accessible, multi-dimensional structures.
3. **The Z-Axis (File Size = Weight/Height):** The byte count of a file is its mass and its height. By sorting files strictly by size, we "scan the horizon" to reveal the tallest piles—the massive compound documents that serve as the foundational "posts" of the paddock.

## II. THE TAXONOMY OF MATTER
To crystallize the project, we must filter the raw digital flux into structural discrete geometries.

### The Ground & The Noise
*These elements are not "bad"; they are the foundational earth of the paddock, but must be cleared from the horizon.*
- **Dung & Silt:** Fragmented ideas, loose notes, and early drafts that provide nutrients but lack structure. (To be composted into deep archives).
- **Dross, Toss, & Floss:** Redundant copies, unaligned non-primes, and superficial stylistic noise that clutters the true variables. 

### The Crystalline Geometries
*The surviving elements that have passed through the Root 42 filter.*
- **Perfect Polygons (Chapters):** A single, mathematically sealed face of the narrative. A complete, unbroken loop of logic and story.
- **Perfect Solids (Books):** Sets of perfect polygons assembled into a stable, 3D structure that can hold volume (The Icosahedron, The 93-Point Solid).
- **The Series (Tessellations/Sets):** Perfect sequences of solids expanding to fill the pan-dimensional space.

## III. THE PROTOCOL OF THE PADDOCK

We must establish "what we even have here" by conducting a raw quantitative scan of the paddock. 
- Map every item by its Z-axis (size).
- Identify the highest "posts" (the massive compound documents like the 100,000+ word solids). 
- Plot the geographic distribution of these posts.

### Step 2: Isolation and Dating of Non-Primes
- Iterate through the lower-lying structures.
- Date and identify all "non-prime" files (redundancies, permutations, iterative noise).
- Extract these from the active staging ground and bury them in the soil (`LIBRARY_ARCHIVES`) so they do not cast shadows on the horizon.

### Step 3: The Root 42 Crystallization Filter & Tagging
- Audit the "posts" (the Solids) and the remaining "polygons" (chapters) against the **Root 42/51 Constants**.
- Is the structure aligned? Does it possess the $540^\circ$ Möbius Torsion? Does it resonate with the 5-12-13 aperture?
