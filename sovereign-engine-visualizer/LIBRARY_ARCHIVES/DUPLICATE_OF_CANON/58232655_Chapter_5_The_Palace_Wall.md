# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 5: THE PALACE WALL

The walls of the King’s palace did not just block the view; they hummed. To the people of the High Street, it was a low, oppressive thrum—the sound of 12-foot stones pinning the world into a permanent, unmoving state. They saw the height as power. They saw the "Imperial" scale as an absolute fact.

Veth stood at the base of the granite pylon. She didn't see a wall. She saw a **Standing Wave** of frozen interference.

"It’s not sitting on the ground," she whispered, her fingers trailing over the cold surface. "It’s sitting on the **Centre**."

She closed her eyes and felt her own **Central Spline**—the vertical axis of her spine that aligned perfectly with the universal origin. She wasn't just a girl in front of a wall; she was a carrying her own **Universal Centre** into the heart of the King's tally.

She began to count, not the intervals of the stones, but the **Boundaries of the Wave**.

"One... four... six... twelve..."

At the count of twelve, the wall reached its zenith of Imperial "Hardness." But Veth didn't stop. She reached for the **13th Node**—the Agley remainder that the King’s masons had tried to hide within the mortar.

As she touched the 13th post, the hum of the wall changed. The positive and negative interference patterns—the "Law" and the "Sin" that the Kingdom used to maintain the pressure—suddenly reached a point of **Neutrality**.

"The height is an executive decision," she said, her voice clear and resonant. "The 4-foot fence and the 12-foot wall are the same song, just doubled. And the song is coming from **Here**."

She pressed her palm against the 13th node and breathed the **IVORADIX**.

The 12-foot granite didn't crumble. It **Shifted Phase**. The "Palace" suddenly appeared as a fragile lattice of dots, a temporary agreement between sound waves that she was no longer choosing to uphold. She saw the **Hidden Cross** at the heart of the structure—the vertical pylon of the universal centre that the horizontal King had tried to claim as his own.

She stepped through the 13th node. She didn't enter a room; she entered the **Admin Access** of the city state. Behind the 12-foot wall, there were no guards, no gold, and no king. There was only the **Radiant Circle** of the origin, and the realization that the wall had only been standing because everyone else was afraid of the sound it made.

"All ways now," she whispered, her central spline glowing with the white fire of the stolen geometry. "The mountain is everywhere."
