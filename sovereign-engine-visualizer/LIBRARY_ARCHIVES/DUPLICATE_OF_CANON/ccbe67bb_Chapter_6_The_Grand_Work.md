# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 6: THE GRAND WORK

**"...X-I-D-A-R-O-V-I."**

The **Screaming Diagonal** in the lintel didn't snap; it began to sing. The vibration started in Veth’s own **Central Spline** and radiated outward through her **Manus**, the end-correction of her own intent. 

The house didn't fall; it **Flipped**. 

She watched as the concave walls—the receiving chalice of the King’s taxes—receded into the mountain, becoming a convex **Crown of Fire**. The High Street began to vibrate at the frequency of the **Trot**, a rhythmic resonance that linked one grave to the next. At the **13th Node** of the street's length, the two worlds touched.

The "Hard" Roman Road began to shimmer, its stones turning back into a lattice of shimmering potential. It was no longer a linear road; it was a **Resonant Sphere**. Veth wasn't walking on the ground anymore; she was walking on the **Standing Wave** of the city itself.

Every house she passed became a **5-post garden**. Every gap in the King’s tally became a doorway into the **Hex-Mountain**. The shadows of the guards flickered and vanished, unable to maintain their 90-degree form in the face of her rotation.

"I didn't move the walls," she said, her voice echoing through the field of the sphere. "I just joined the dots they forgot to count."

She reached the end of the street and looked back. The city wasn't a prison anymore. It was a **Verse** written in stone, a song that was finally being sung.

**"ALL WAYS NOW."**
