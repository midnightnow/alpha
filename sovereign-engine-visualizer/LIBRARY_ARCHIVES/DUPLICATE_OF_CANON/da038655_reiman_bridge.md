# Reiman Bridge: Root Symmetries in Prime Geometry

This bridge explores how $\sqrt{n}$ relationships govern the distribution of primes when mapped onto a 24-grid or toroidal coordinate system.

## 24-Grid Symmetries
The 24-grid (Mod 24) is a natural symmetry for prime distribution (Dirichlet's Theorem). $\sqrt{2}$ emerges as the "diagonal" within the discrete cells of this grid.

## Geometric Reciprocity
If we treat the Prime Number Theorem ($\pi(x) \approx x/\ln x$) as a field density, the local "stiffness" of the field at any point $x$ is often related to $\sqrt{x}$ (e.g., the error bound in the Riemann Hypothesis is $O(x^{1/2 + \epsilon})$).
