# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 13: THE SILENCE OF THE NEIGHBORS

Kaelen walked through the settlement on the Blue Terrace. It was a grid of clean, efficient huts, each built to the Council’s "Managed Standard." On the surface, it looked like a community. But the Hiss was louder here—the sound of ten thousand unspoken words.

He stopped at a small garden where two men sat on a bench. They lived in adjacent huts. They shared a fence. But they sat five feet apart, as if separated by an invisible valve.

"Nice weather for the rendering," the man on the left said. He wore the white apron of a Baker, his hands dusted with the remnants of the Council's processed flour.

"A bit high in the humidity range," the man on the right replied. He wore the spectacles of a Diagnostic Specialist. "Good for the spores. Bad for the retinas."

Kaelen stood before them. "You’ve lived next to each other for a year," he said. "Do you know his name?"

The Baker hesitated. "He's the Doctor. From Sector 9."

"And he's the Baker," the Doctor added, his voice clipped. "He provides the carbohydrate quota for the block."

"Is that all you are to each other?" Kaelen asked. "Organs in a Leviathan?"

The Doctor sighed, adjusting his lenses. "It's difficult, Kaelen. We don't speak the same language. When I talk about the 'Systemic Equilibrium of the Liver,' he looks at me as if I’m speaking Latin. Which I am. And when he talks about 'The Proofing Cycle of the Dough,' I find it… functionally irrelevant to my specialty."

"But you’re both humans," Kaelen said. "Do you not share a common life?"

"We share the politics of the Council," the Baker said, his face darkening. "The Doctor believes the grain quota should be docked to increase the antiseptic supply. He thinks the 'Bladder' of the city is more important than the 'Stomach'."

"And the Baker believes that 'Flow' is more important than 'Safety'," the Doctor snapped. "He wants to use wild yeast. He doesn't understand that and un-monitored fermentation is a theological threat to the Grid."

They turned away from each other, back to the "Safe Summary" of the weather.

"What is good for the Liver is not good for the Bladder," the Doctor muttered.

Kaelen looked at their hands. The Baker’s hands were white with dust; the Doctor’s were stained with ink. Neither could do what the other did. Neither *wanted* to. They had accepted the "Docking" of their souls in exchange for the comfort of their specialties.

"The Council didn't just build the walls of your huts," Kaelen said. "They built the walls of your heads. You disagree on God and State because you are no longer looking at the same world. You are looking at two different fragments of a broken mirror."

Tal (He Brews) appeared at the edge of the path, carrying a jug of the bitter wort. He didn't offer them a "Summary." He offered them a **Drink**.

"This is the brew of the Whole Man," Tal said, placing the jug on the bench between them. "It contains the grain he grew and the medicine he needs. It's not for the liver or the stomach. It's for the **Heart**."

The neighbors looked at the jug. They looked at each other. For a moment, the valve flickered.

"Go on," Kaelen said. "Break the silence. Before the Silt in your throats becomes a Grave."
