### CHAPTER 3: A CONSTELLATION OF JARS

A Nomad drifted in a violet haze, moving like a shark in a deep pool. A form held no fixed boundary; an outline shimmered, perpetually recalculating on ambient entropy. Kaelen laboured, keeping pace. Heavy boots, forged for a hexagonal framework of Sector 36, felt numbly irrelevant on a smooth probability field.

"Halt walking on pins," a Nomad hummed. No vocal audio; a direct injection of meaning in Kaelen's neural web. "No pins here. Only lines. Only flow."

Kaelen halted. He looked down. He labored on discrete logic—*here*, then *there*. A Horizon was a continuous ribbon. No stepping on a line; a man had to surf.

"Reveal," Kaelen breathed.

A Nomad raised a palm. A violet hue parted.

Far in a deep dark, floating like air caverns in a void ocean, hung **Jars**.

Kaelen realized viewing a City he had fled—a flickering shape of pale light. Near hovered others. A cube of burning red geometry. A globe of liquid mercury. A folding tesseract.

"Phantoms," Kaelen mumbled.

"Pools," a Nomad corrected. "Each Jar is a Pool of laws. Your City was a Callable Pool ($\aleph_0$). Integers. Primes. Grids. You count a block. You name a man."

A Nomad pointed on a dark, whirling jar near. A black cloud locked in glass.

"Uncallable ($\aleph_1$). No grid. No atom. Pure field physics. Plunging in, you dissolve. You are too rigid."

Kaelen felt a chill. "How many?"

"Boundless," a Nomad hummed. "Not all fathomless deeps are equal. Here—" a palm swept a horizon—"is a **Power Pool**. For every active City, a Pool of all possible variations blooms. Some empty. Some full. Some... hungry."

Kaelen's Ophanim Toolkit rumbled in a palm. He looked down. A pane flickered.

> **ERROR:** ROOT_FUNCTION_DEPRECATED
> **ATTEMPTING TO MEASURE GAP...**
> **INPUT:** $\sqrt{225}$
> **OUTPUT:** NULL

"Broken," Kaelen mumbled. "Built for Roots. For Primes. Finding a square root of a gap, but no squares remain here."

A Nomad drifted near. "You measure water applying a hard comb. You need a flow meter."

Kaelen tapped a pane. He accessed a core code of an Ophanim. He found old anchors: `ROOT_05`, `ROOT_17`, `STEP_ALIGNMENT`. Greyed out. Blocked by a void.

He needed a new harmonic. No root. An **Integral**.

He began typing, replacing hard logic with purely continuous variational math. A pane flared, no longer hunting coordinates; rendering a field of minimal action.

A toolkit hummed. Flickering halted. A ribbon of golden light fired out, not a rigid beam, curving gently around pockets of deep entropy. A **Geodesic**—a shortest path in curving geometry.

"Functional," Kaelen said.

"For now," a Nomad warned. "Look deeper on a Jars."

Kaelen followed a golden ribbon. A line led on a specific Jar. Dark, barely visible, cradled between a red cube and a mercury globe.

"There," a Nomad hummed. "A **Null Pool**."

Kaelen frowned. "Empty?"

"Harder," a Nomad hummed. "A Pool containing a paradox. Archons dump an error they cannot purge. Glitches. Men breaking a cage but forgetting human marrow."

A Nomad turned on Kaelen. A face finally held firm—a perfect, smooth oval.

"You fled an Integer Vault, Kaelen. A Horizon harbors predators. Entities here feed on Logic. Hunting a man imposing order on a Continuum."

"Like me," Kaelen hummed.

"Like you," a Nomad confirmed. "You are a walking axiom. Out here, axioms equal bait."

Kaelen locked eyes on a toolkit. A golden ribbon pulsed. Showing a way forward. Broadcasting a location.

"I need hiding cover," Kaelen murmured.

"No hiding in a Continuum," a Nomad hummed. "You only **Integrate**. Become a fraction of a whole. Stop fighting an entropy. Let a void flow."

Kaelen looked on a dark Jar. A Null Pool. He felt a pull. A recognition.

"Step 226," Kaelen mumbled.

He adapted a toolkit. He abandoned minimizing an action. He maximized an **Entropy Factor**. He made himself a noisy variable. He made himself uncertain.

A golden ribbon faded. Kaelen blurred.

"Fine," a Nomad hummed. "Now you are an unknown. No longer a beacon."

Moving forward, plunging in a deep dark between burning orbs.
