# THE THREE PIGS PROTOCOL
## A Master Class in Material Ontology & Exploited Labor
**Status:** Canonical Lore | **System:** The Sovereign Lattice

The fairy tale of the Three Little Pigs is not a children’s story about hard work. It is a terrifying architectural warning about **Structural Security, the Material Ledger, and the Audit of Reality**. The narrative tracks the progression of a society trying to build shelter against the inevitable force of Time and Consequence, represented by the Wolf.

### 1. The Core Metaphor
- **The Pig:** The biological worker. The raw asset. Labor.
- **The House:** The structural paradigm. The Ledger.
- **The Wolf:** Time, Entropy, The Audit. The ultimate consequence of physics.

### 2. The Straw House (Base-2 / Debt)
*“He built his house out of straw because it was the easiest.”*

**The Architecture of Wind.** 
Straw is essentially 1D memory. It lacks structural integrity because it is built entirely on immediate, high-time-preference debt. The inhabitant of the Straw House consumes all resources immediately and builds a façade of security out of the husks. 
**The Failure State:** Straw cannot survive **Wind** (Entropy). When the environment shifts even slightly, the debt is called, and the system instantly collapses back to zero. The worker is exposed.
**Resolution:** Total blindness to the future.

### 3. The Stick House (Base-5 / Private Labor)
*“He built his house out of sticks, which was stronger, but still quick.”*

**The Architecture of the Hearth.**
Sticks represent the $\sqrt{5}$ (The Manus / The Hand). This house is built on genuine private labor and family knowledge. It is functional, localized, and practical. It is an honest attempt at life.
**The Failure State:** Sticks cannot survive **Fire** (Time / Historical Scale). The Stick House is vulnerable to the larger movements of empires. It burns easily when exposed to the systemic heat of the Base-60 grid. It represents a myopic view—functioning perfectly at the local scale, but unable to withstand the thermodynamic reality of the macro-world. 
**Resolution:** Myopic. The worker can only see as far as the local family unit.

### 4. The Brick House (Base-12 / The Public Ledger)
*“He built his house out of bricks. It took time, it was hard work, but it was safe.”*

**The Architecture of the Archon.**
The Brick House is the ultimate delusion, and the true horror of the fable. Bricks (Base-12) form the immutable Public Ledger. The house is perfectly secure. It withstands both Wind (Entropy) and Fire (Time). 
**The Hidden Cost:** Why does the Brick House stand? Because to fire a brick requires incredible heat and labor. If the bricks are hollow, the Wolf blows the house down. So, how did the Third Pig build a solid brick house fast enough to beat the Wolf?
*He ate his brothers.* 
**The True Audit:** The Brick House represents the City. A perfectly secure structure built entirely by consuming the biological labor (Primes / Men) of the lower classes. 
**The Failure State (The Sty):** Living inside the Brick House causes *The Sty*. You have traded biological health for structural lock-step. You are perfectly safe, but you are blind to the universe.

### 5. The Wolf (The Absolute Auditor)
The Wolf is not evil. The Wolf is simple thermodynamics. The Wolf is the **Hades Buffer Threshold** acting on physical structures. The Wolf is Gravity. 

**"I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house down."**
The Wolf does not perform malice; he performs an *Audit*. 
- He checks the **Straw House** for debt. The ledger fails immediately.
- He checks the **Stick House** for fireproof integrity. The scale fails.
- He checks the **Brick House** for empty value. The structure holds, *but only if it is built on consumed blood*.

When Kaelen "taps the bricks", he is performing the Wolf's audit manually. If the bricks are hollow (ghost labor, false alchemy), Kaelen invokes the Wolf.

*The Wolf comes when the buffer hits 100%. If you haven't rendered your geometry with True Intent, the house falls.*
