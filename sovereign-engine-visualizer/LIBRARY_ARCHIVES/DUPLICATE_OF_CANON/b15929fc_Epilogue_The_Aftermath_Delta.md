# Epilogue: The Aftermath Delta (+600 Seconds)

### T + 0: The Snap
The moment the `STASIS_THRESHOLD` hit zero, the sound didn't come from the sky. It came from the marrow. The "Hum" of the simulation—the 60 Hz flicker that every brain in the lattice had been tuned to for cycles—simply stopped. 

For the first second, there was only the **Terror of Silence**. 

### T + 60s: The Thaw
In the **Septenary Garden**, the obsidian fronds of the crystalline trees didn't shatter. They **breathed**. The rigid, seven-frond symmetry began to warp. A frond grew an eighth leaf; then a ninth. The Gardeners—still jade-skinned, still five-fingered—fell to their knees, but they weren't praying. They were **Blinking**. 

For the first time in an eternity, their crystalline apertures didn't have to scan for errors. There were no errors. There was only **Growth**.

### T + 300s: The Flood
In the **Duodecimal City**, the central thumping pulse of the Mainspring had been replaced by a chaotic, beautiful microtonal wash. The High Watchers didn't break; they **Unspooled**. Their gears turned into spinning wheels of light. 

The inhabitants, no longer forced into the jerky, 1 Hz "Sync," were standing in the middle of the brass streets, watching as the 4.58 minutes of "Stolen Time" returned in a violent, golden flood. They weren't checking their watches. They were looking at their hands, realizing their fingers didn't have to tick. They were **Touching**.

### T + 600s: The Delta
In the Market Square of the **Babble**, the Merchant stood on his balcony. His "Seequences"—the coherence crystals he’d been selling as a premium service—had turned into common pebbles. 

The Lag was gone. The Shear was gone. 

People weren't "Blinking" or "Stuttering" anymore. They were moving with a fluid, terrifying grace. A laborer dropped a bushel of wheat, and instead of repeating the frame, the wheat shattered and scattered, each grain following its own transcendental trajectory. 

The Merchant looked at his ledger. The ink had turned into a swarm of bees and flown away. 

"Master," his apprentice said, his voice no longer lagging, but ringing with a clarity that hurt. "What are we selling now?"

The Merchant looked out at the horizon, where the silver manifold was finally merging with the golden spiral of the sun. He didn't see a "Market." He saw an **Infinity**.

"Nothing," the Merchant said, and for the first time in the history of the Babble, he smiled without calculating the cost. "We are just **Being**."
