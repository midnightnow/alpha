## 2. Local Shear States

The stability of any local node is determined by the **Shear Delta ($s$)**, the difference between Operator Intent and Environmental Entropy.

*   **THE LAG ($s < 0.05$):** Temporal stuttering. Frames drop or repeat. High latency in physical movement and speech.
*   **THE FLOW ($0.05 \leq s \leq 0.25$):** Stable rendering. The "sweet spot" where consciousness cohers and transactions execute. 
*   **THE GLITCH ($s > 0.25$):** Critical de-rendering. Wireframe leakage. Hard-edges of the Source Code bleed through the texture of reality.

## 3. Economic Concepts

*   **Coherence Debt:** The accumulated thermodynamic "tax" an entity owes the system. If Debt > Coherence, the entity enters a permanent GLITCH state.
*   **Smoothing Protocol:** A stability-as-a-service product (sold by the Merchant) that temporarily lowers local shear.
*   **Noise Mask:** A high-entropy injection that makes an entity "invisible" to Lattice scanners by mimicking background clutter.
*   **Remainder Coins:** Physical shards of the $0.0191$ drift. The new currency of the Babble. Unlike Stasis Credits, their value is determined by their "Error Density."

## 4. Systemic Phenomena

*   **Sync-Hammer:** A high-frequency pulse from the Lattice designed to "Re-Zero" a district back to $0.1237$ (The Old Order).
*   **1/n Overflow:** A defensive maneuver that inverts a Sync-Hammer. By forcing the system to process infinite noise ($n \to 0$), the attacker crashes the local Lattice node.
*   **The Kaelen Signature (Iteration 045):** The active remains of the original Navigator. Manifests as a static point of infinite noise in the Source Code.
