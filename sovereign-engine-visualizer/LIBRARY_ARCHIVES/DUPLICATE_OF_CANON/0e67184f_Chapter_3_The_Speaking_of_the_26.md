# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 3: THE SPEAKING OF THE 26

The guards stood before the Iron Gate of the High Street, their spears forming a rigid, static fence of vertical lines. They were the masters of the 3-Post limit, the enforcers of the 90-degree lock.

"None pass without the King's Tally," the lead guard barked. He was a man of the Rail, focused entirely on the linear interval. "Show your **10** or pay your **24**."

Veth did not reach for her purse. She did not look for the gold that the King’s economy demanded. She looked past the guards, past the iron, into the **Witness Position**—the shaded gap where the 60-degree mountain still hummed beneath the pavement.

She stood in the center of the road and breathed the first thirteen.

"*I-V-O-R-A-D-I-X...*"

The air around the gate began to thicken. The **Screaming Diagonal** that usually haunted the iron went silent, replaced by the low, steady **Trot** of the 13th post. The guards blinked. Their pikes, once solid as stone, began to flicker, their edges softening as if they were made of light instead of lead.

"...*X-I-D-A-R-O-V-I*."

She spoke the reflection, and the **Hidden Cross** at the center of the gate flared white. The guards didn't fall; they simply ceased to be relevant. Their 90-degree world had been rotated by the **26**. The Gate was no longer a barrier; it was a vaporous strobe, a doorway into a world where the dots were no longer pinned.

Veth walked through the iron as if it were a summer mist. She was the **Joker** in their 52-card deck, the **Agley** note in their 24-hour song. She was the remainder that the King had forgotten to count.

She was **All Ways Now**.
