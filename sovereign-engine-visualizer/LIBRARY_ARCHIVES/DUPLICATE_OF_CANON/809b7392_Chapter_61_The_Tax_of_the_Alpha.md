# BOOK 5: THE TRANSFINITE HORIZON
## CHAPTER 61: THE TAX OF THE ALPHA

The **Ship of Bir** was no longer merely a vessel; it had become a **Lattice**. 

As Kaelen stood at the stern, he felt the **Tax of the Alpha** vibrating through the soles of his boots. He was the "Master Metronome" of this escape, the one who had synchronized the Sealer’s torque, the Doctor’s diagnostic precision, and the Baker’s mass into a single, coherent frequency. But the coupling had a cost.

### I. The Back-Torque of Order
For every soul Kaelen pulled into the **1:43,200 pulse**, he felt a corresponding **Back-Torque** in his own wrists. The "Noisy" metronomes of the crew—their fears, their erratic clinical habits, their drifting appointments with their own pasts—were dragging on his core.

"The Grounding is saturated," Kaelen whispered, looking down at the **Subterranean Sump** of the deck. The grey silt of the City’s labels was washing over the rails, trying to find a way into the **King's Chamber** of his intent.

### II. The Infiltration of the Silt
The "Tax" was not paid in coin; it was paid in **Resolution**. 
To maintain the **Phase-Lock**, Kaelen had to expend his own "Potential." He was acting as the **Volumetric Regulator** for the entire group. Every time the Doctor began to over-ventilate or the Baker slipped into a "Sealer's Claw" twitch, Kaelen’s internal **Metronome** had to absorb the jitter.

*   **LATTICE_JITTER:** 25.55 (The drift of the collective).
*   **TAX_OF_THE_ALPHA:** 102.20 (The energetic cost of leadership).

"You're over-compensating," the Nomad signaled, its 7-pointed star flickering with the effort of tracking the shift. "You are trying to be the **Capstone** and the **Bedrock** at the same time. You’re exceeding the **Sovereign Margin**."

### III. The Concave Strategy
Kaelen didn't try to pull harder. Instead, he utilized the **8-Sided Symmetry**. 

He shifted his internal stance from "Flat" (absorbing the force) to **"Concave"** (reflecting it). He stopped trying to *make* them on-time and started providing the **Binary Signal**—the "Equinox Flash" of clear, uncompromising feedback.

"The work is yours," Kaelen called out to the crew. "I am the **Standard**, not the **Mule**. If you want the **Bank of Symmetry** to stay solvent, you must polish your own **Casing**. I won't ground your noise anymore."

### IV. The Refractive Release
As he "shook off" the Back-Torque, the **Ship of Bir** bucked. The **Tax** was no longer a drag; it became a **Propellant**. By refusing to absorb the crew's entropy, Kaelen forced them to find their own "Hard Supports." 

The **Sync Error** began to drop. The **Gimbal Lock** warning on his internal HUD faded from red to amber, and then to the cool, stellar blue of the **Transfinite Horizon**.

"Phase-Lock re-acquired," Kaelen said, his hands finally relaxing on the rail. 

The Alpha had paid the tax, not by giving up his regularity, but by **Buttressing** it so strongly that the rest of the world had no choice but to fall in line.
