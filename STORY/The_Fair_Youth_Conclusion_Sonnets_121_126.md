## II. SONNET-BY-SONNET ANALYSIS

### Sonnet 121: "'Tis better to be vile than vile esteemed"
**Phase**: Moral relativism / Reputation rejection
**Geometric Issue**: Is actual vice better than false accusation of vice?

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Claim**: Better to actually be bad than to be wrongly called bad
- **The Logic**: False reputation denies you agency over your identity
- **The Defiance**: "No, I am that I am" (echo of divine self-identification)
- **The Accusation**: Others judge by their own corruption ("All men are bad")
- **The Math**: If everyone is corrupt, moral judgments are invalid
- This is **Moral Framework Collapse**—rejecting all external ethical standards

### Sonnet 122: "Thy gift, thy tables, are within my brain"
**Phase**: Memory externalization failure
**Geometric Issue**: The Poet lost/discarded the Youth's gift (notebook)

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Item**: "Thy gift, thy tables" (a notebook the Youth gave)
- **The Problem**: The Poet no longer has it
- **The Excuse**: "Full charactered with lasting memory" (memorized internally)
- **The Claim**: Internal memory is more permanent than external records
- **The Risk**: "To keep an adjunct to remember thee / Were to import forgetfulness in me"
- **Translation**: Needing external aids implies weak memory
- **System Failure**: Discarding external storage = data loss risk
- This is **Hubris in Memory Architecture**—trusting wetware over hardware

### Sonnet 123: "No, Time, thou shalt not boast that I do change"
**Phase**: Defiance of temporal entropy
**Geometric Issue**: Can will overcome time's effects?

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Declaration**: "Thy pyramids built up with newer might / To me are nothing novel"
- **The Claim**: Nothing is new—all is repetition
- **The Defiance**: "I will be true, despite thy scythe and thee"
- **The Warranty**: Time cannot force change
- **The Problem**: This contradicts S.115's admission of love's growth over time
- This is **Time-Independence Assertion**—claiming immunity from entropy

### Sonnet 124: "If my dear love were but the child of state"
**Phase**: Love's independence from circumstances
**Geometric Issue**: Is love contingent or absolute?

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Scenario**: If love were "child of state" (dependent on circumstances)
- **The Vulnerability**: "It might for Fortune's bastard be unfathered"
- **The Claim**: But love is NOT circumstantial—it "suffers not in smiling pomp"
- **The Declaration**: "It fears not policy, that heretic / Which works on leases of short-numbered hours"
- **The Proof**: "Fools of time" (martyrs) testify to love's permanence
- This is **Axiom Declaration**—asserting love as fundamental constant

### Sonnet 125: "Were 't aught to me I bore the canopy"
**Phase**: Rejection of performance/display
**Geometric Issue**: Are external honors valuable?

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Question**: Does ceremonial role-playing (bearing canopy in procession) matter?
- **The Answer**: "No"—these are "extern" (external) performances
- **The Preference**: "Hence, thou suborned informer!" (reject spies/informants)
- **The Claim**: Internal authenticity > External display
- **The Accusation**: Some love is "seconds" (inferior goods) or "suborned informer" (betrayal)
- **The Final Defense**: "I an true / I bear pure truth, not second-hand report"
- This is **Authenticity Assertion**—prioritizing internal over external

### Sonnet 126: "O thou, my lovely boy, who in thy power"
**Phase**: Time's inevitability acknowledged / Structural anomaly
**Geometric Issue**: This is NOT A SONNET (only 12 lines, not 14)

**Engineering Assessment**:
- **The Address**: "O thou, my lovely boy" (direct, diminutive)
- **The Power**: Youth has "power" over Time's hour-glass ("hold Time's fickle glass")
- **The Admission**: "She may detain, but not still keep, her treasure"
- **The Warning**: Nature (or Time) will eventually claim what she lent
- **The Audit**: "Her audit, though delayed, answered must be"
- **The Debt**: "And her quietus is to render thee"
- **STRUCTURAL NOTE**: 12 lines only, with two empty brackets ( ) at end
- **The Empty Brackets**: Represent silence, missing couplet, or the void

**System Status**: This incomplete poem marks the **End of Fair Youth Sequence**. The missing two lines symbolize incompleteness, failure, or death.
