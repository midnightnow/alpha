# Hades Gap Taxonomy (Ψ)

The Principia Mathematica Geometrica recognizes that the "slop" or "mandatory breath" of a system manifests at different scales and in different domains. This taxonomy differentiates the three primary expressions of the Hades Gap constant.

## 1. Ψ_material / Ψ_narrative : 12.37%
**Domain**: Narrative tension, biological breath debt, physical paving.
**Derivation**: Refined vertex-to-node ratio or $1 - 1/e$ approximation in specific damping fields.
**Meaning**: The "Mandatory Mercy" that prevents a system from reaching absolute, dead coherence (petrification). In the Shakespearean Sonnets, this is the gap between the ideal "Fair Youth" and the actual entropy of flesh.

## 2. Ψ_vertex : 12.90%
**Domain**: Structural icosahedral lattice (93-Faced Solid).
**Derivation**: $12 \text{ vertices} / 93 \text{ total nodes} \approx 0.1290$.
**Meaning**: The structural ratio of the "anchors" (vertices) to the total informational content of the lattice. This is the unrefined geometric weight of the enclosure nodes.

## 3. Ψ_structure / Ψ_flux : 16.67%
**Domain**: Projective mapping, 2D-to-3D transformation.
**Derivation**: $4 \text{ squares} / 24 \text{ squares} \text{ in a square-wheel mapping to } 20 \text{ icosahedral faces}$.
**Meaning**: The gaps or "slits" created when folding a higher-cardinality 2D plane into a lower-cardinality 3D volume. These represent the points of high information flux or "the teeth" of the geometry where the 5-fold and 4-fold systems grind against each other.
