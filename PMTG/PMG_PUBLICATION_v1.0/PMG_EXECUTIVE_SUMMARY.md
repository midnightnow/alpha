### The Question

Why does the Latin alphabet have 26 letters? Why does the day have 24 hours? Why does the year have 365 days?

These numbers feel arbitrary — cultural accidents, historical contingencies. This monograph demonstrates that they are not. They are the **local solutions** of a geometric system that begins with nothing more than counting and a right angle.

### The Starting Conditions

We begin with five axioms:

1. **Succession** — from any count $n$, produce $n+1$.
2. **Unit** — the smallest count is 1.
3. **Boundary** — $n$ intervals require $n+1$ boundaries (the "Ghost Post").
4. **Closure** — counting extends to two dimensions via the right angle.
5. **Bilateral symmetry** — embodied observers double their structures (left/right).

From these, the following emerges by logical necessity:

- The **3‑4‑5 triangle** — the minimal integer right closure.
- The **5‑12‑13 triangle** — the Ghost Post of the 12‑cycle under the utilitarian choice of base‑12.
- The **10‑24‑26 manifold** — the bilateral doubling: 10 fingers, 24 hours, 26 letters.

No additional assumptions are required. No cultural knowledge is imported. The alphabet's capacity, the day's length, and the counting base arise from the geometry of the right angle applied to the Ghost Post of a highly composite cycle.

### What This Framework Claims

The PMG claims that **geometry provides a socket** — a structural capacity — that human‑scale conventions happen to fill. The 26‑letter alphabet fits because the Ghost Post of the 12‑cycle, doubled bilaterally, yields 26. The 365‑day year resonates because the spiral parameters (171 and 23) sum to it. The number 42 appears because it is what remains when you subtract the observer and its boundary from the space of possibilities.

These are structural observations, not causal claims. The year is 365 days because of orbital mechanics. The alphabet has 26 letters because of historical convention. But the fact that **the same numbers emerge from pure counting geometry** — independently of physics, independently of history — is the phenomenon this monograph investigates.

---

### What This Framework Does Not Claim

1. It does not claim that base‑12 is the only viable counting base.
2. It does not claim that the alphabet *had* to have 26 letters.
3. It does not claim predictive power over physical phenomena.
4. It does not claim that ancient civilizations encoded these numbers intentionally.
5. It does not claim that the geometry *caused* the alphabet or the calendar.

The framework is **synthetic** — its truth is coherence, not correspondence. It is a geometric poem: internally consistent, mathematically verifiable, and honest about its choices.
