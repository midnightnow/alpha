# Velocity Delta Simulation: The Phase-Shift Zone

In our **Antigravity Builder**, we move from the static grid to the kinetic field. This simulation document maps the behavior of the "Incommensurable Moment"—the threshold where the corners of the square core effectively outrun the gravitational grid lines.

## The Theory of the Displacement Zone

As the square rotates, the corners (Radius $\approx 0.707$) maintain a tangential velocity significantly higher than the midpoints (Radius $0.5$). 

### The Shear Layer
The volume of space between the internal circle (Radius $0.5$) and the external circle (Radius $0.707$) is the **Shear Layer**. Within this layer, the material is technically part of the same "solid" object but is experiencing vastly different inertial forces.

### The Incommensurable Moment
When the rotation frequency ($\omega$) reaches a value where the corner velocity hits a harmonic of the diagonal ($\sqrt{2}$), the corners " ruptures" from the $1 \times 1$ rational grid. 

* **The Result:** The mass is no longer indexed to a static point in 3D space.
* **The Effect:** Local gravitational acceleration ($g$) cannot "grab" the mass because the reference points (the corners) are cycling through the diagonal vector faster than the field can reconcile.
