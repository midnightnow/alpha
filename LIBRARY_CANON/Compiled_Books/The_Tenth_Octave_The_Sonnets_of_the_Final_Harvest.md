## II. SONNET-BY-SONNET GEOMETRIC ANALYSIS

### Sonnet 73: "That time of year thou mayst in me behold"
**Phase**: Autumn self-identification / Memento mori
**Geometric Issue**: The Poet maps his aging to three natural cycles:
1. **Autumn** (bare boughs where birds once sang)
2. **Twilight** (after sunset, before night)
3. **Dying fire** (consumed by what once nourished it)

**Engineering Assessment**:
- This is a **Triple Redundant System** for communicating mortality
- **Key Line**: "This thou perceiv'st, which makes thy love more strong / To love that well which thou must leave ere long"
- **The Paradox**: Impending loss increases love intensity
- **Formula**: dL/dt > 0 as t → T (love increases as time decreases)

### Sonnet 74: "But be contented when that fell arrest"
**Phase**: Death preparation / Asset allocation
**Geometric Issue**: What survives the Poet's death?
**The Split**: Body ("the dregs of life") vs. Spirit ("the better part")

**Engineering Assessment**:
- The Poet performs **Partitioning**:
  - Physical form = worthless ("prey of worms")
  - Verse form = valuable ("my spirit is thine")
- This is **Data Duplication**: consciousness copied to non-biological substrate
- **Claim**: "The worth of that is that which it contains" (the verse preserves essence)

### Sonnet 75: "So are you to my thoughts as food to life"
**Phase**: Dependency mapping / Addiction protocol
**Geometric Issue**: The Youth is defined as **Metabolic Necessity**

**Engineering Assessment**:
- Three metaphors stacked:
  1. **Food to Life** (biological necessity)
  2. **Showers to Earth** (agricultural necessity)
  3. **Miser with Gold** (economic hoarding)
- The Poet identifies a **Feast/Famine Cycle**:
  - Doubting → Famine (spiritual starvation)
  - Possessing → Feast (but fear of loss)
  - Neither state is stable
- This is **Oscillatory Dysfunction**—the system cannot achieve equilibrium

### Sonnet 76: "Why is my verse so barren of new pride?"
**Phase**: Creative exhaustion / Style audit
**Geometric Issue**: Why does the Poet's verse lack variety?
**The Answer**: "all my best is dressing old words new"

**Engineering Assessment**:
- The Poet acknowledges **Algorithmic Limitation**
- He has only one theme ("you and love")
- This is **Bandwidth Saturation**—the channel is filled with a single frequency
- **Implication**: The Youth has consumed all creative resources

### Sonnet 77: "Thy glass will show thee how thy beauties wear"
**Phase**: Gift protocol / Memento mori for the Youth
**Geometric Issue**: The Poet gives the Youth a **Blank Book** as mirror of time

**Engineering Assessment**:
- Three tracking devices:
  1. **Glass** (mirror) → Visual decay monitoring
  2. **Dial** (clock) → Temporal decay monitoring
  3. **Vacant leaves** (blank book) → Memory decay monitoring
- This is **Distributed Surveillance**—the Youth must log his own entropy
- **The Cruelty**: The gift is a constant reminder of mortality

### Sonnet 78: "So oft have I invoked thee for my Muse"
**Phase**: Rival Poet returns / Acknowledgment of competition
**Geometric Issue**: Others now write about the Youth

**Engineering Assessment**:
- The Poet admits the Youth "art all my art"
- But others have learned to use the same source code
- **The Problem**: Open-source Youth → Multiple encoders
- **Tensegrity Failure**: The anchor point now supports many threads

### Sonnet 79: "Whilst I alone did call upon thy aid"
**Phase**: Exclusivity loss / Market saturation
**Geometric Issue**: "My verse alone had all thy gentle grace"—**past tense**

**Engineering Assessment**:
- The Poet traces the arc:
  1. Monopoly period (only his verse)
  2. Innovation period (he "taught" the Youth to inspire others)
  3. Competition period (now others write "better")
- **The Irony**: The Poet's success (elevating the Youth) creates his failure (rivals)
- This is **The Open Protocol Problem**: Making something famous makes it public

### Sonnet 80: "O, how I faint when I of you do write"
**Phase**: Scale anxiety / Inadequacy protocol
**Geometric Issue**: The Rival is a "better spirit" with a "prouder sail"

**Engineering Assessment**:
- Naval metaphor stack:
  - Poet = small boat ("saucy bark")
  - Rival = tall ship ("goodly pride")
  - Youth = ocean ("wide as the ocean is")
- **The Admission**: "I am a worthless boat"
- **The Plea**: Even if wrecked, the Poet's "love was my decay"
- This is **Catastrophic Self-Assessment** disguised as humility
